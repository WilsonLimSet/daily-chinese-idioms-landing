**Pronunciation:** *wú yǔ lún bǐ*  
**Literal meaning:** Nothing to compare with

## Origin & Usage

This idiom states that nothing (无) exists to (与) compare (伦比) with something, indicating supreme excellence. The character 伦 relates to categories or ranks, suggesting that the subject transcends normal classification. The phrase appeared in Han Dynasty texts praising exceptional individuals or achievements that stood beyond comparison. It represents the highest form of praise in Chinese rhetoric. Modern usage describes outstanding quality, performance, or achievement that cannot be matched by peers or competitors.

## Examples

**English:** "Her performance was incomparable, far surpassing all others."

**Chinese:** 她的表演无与伦比，远超其他人。
