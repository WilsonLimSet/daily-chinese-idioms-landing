**Pronunciation:** *gè yǒu suǒ cháng*  
**Literal meaning:** Each has their strengths

## Origin & Usage

This idiom states that each person (各) has (有) their own (所) strengths (长). It acknowledges that different individuals excel in different areas, promoting appreciation for diversity of talents. The phrase appeared in Confucian texts discussing how wise rulers recognize and employ varied talents. It counters the tendency to rank people hierarchically. Modern usage encourages recognizing everyone's unique contributions and building teams that leverage complementary strengths.

## Examples

**English:** "Both candidates bring different but valuable skills to the position."

**Chinese:** 两位候选人各有所长，都能为职位带来价值。
