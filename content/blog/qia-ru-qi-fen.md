**Pronunciation:** *qià rú qí fèn*  
**Literal meaning:** Exactly as its portion

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes being exactly (恰) as (如) its (其) proper portion (分). It depicts behavior or words that are perfectly calibrated to the situation - neither excessive nor insufficient. The phrase values appropriateness and measure. Modern usage describes well-calibrated responses, actions that are perfectly suited to their context.

## Examples

**English:** "His response was perfectly measured and appropriate."

**Chinese:** 他的回应恰如其分。
