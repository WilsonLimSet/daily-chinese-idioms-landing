**Pronunciation:** *dú zhàn áo tóu*  
**Literal meaning:** Alone occupy the turtle's head

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes alone (独) occupying (占) the turtle's (鳌) head (头). In imperial examinations, the top scholar would stand on the carved turtle head at the palace. The phrase represents achieving first place. Modern usage describes being number one, winning top honors in any competition or field.

## Examples

**English:** "She ranked first in the national examination."

**Chinese:** 她在全国考试中独占鳌头。
