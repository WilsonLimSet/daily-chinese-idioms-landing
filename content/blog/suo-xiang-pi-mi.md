**Pronunciation:** *suǒ xiàng pī mǐ*  
**Literal meaning:** Wherever directed, all fall

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes wherever (所) directed (向), all fall (披靡). It depicts an unstoppable force that defeats everything in its path. The phrase was used for victorious armies that conquered all opposition. Modern usage describes overwhelming dominance in any field - sports teams that win every game, businesses that defeat all competitors.

## Examples

**English:** "Their championship team dominated every opponent."

**Chinese:** 他们的冠军球队所向披靡。
