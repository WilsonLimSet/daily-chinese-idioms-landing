**Pronunciation:** *wú xiè kě jī*  
**Literal meaning:** No crack to strike

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes having no (无) crack (懈) that can (可) be struck (击). It depicts something so perfect that no weakness can be found to attack. The phrase was used for impenetrable defenses. Modern usage describes flawless arguments, plans, or performances that leave no opening for criticism or attack.

## Examples

**English:** "Her argument was logically flawless."

**Chinese:** 她的论点无懈可击。
