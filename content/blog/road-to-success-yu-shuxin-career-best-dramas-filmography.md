**Yu Shuxin (虞书欣)** — known internationally as **Esther Yu** — is one of the most consistently rising Chinese actresses of her generation. Born December 18, 1995, in Shanghai, she has built a career that spans idol pop, fantasy romance, and now, with **Road to Success (灿如繁星)**, a deliberate move toward modern dramatic material.

If you're discovering Yu Shuxin through *Road to Success* — or if you've been following her since *Love Between Fairy and Devil* and want a guide to her full catalog — here's the complete picture of her career so far, and what makes the upcoming sports romance her most ambitious project yet.

## Background and Early Life

Yu Shuxin grew up in Shanghai. After her early education, she moved to Singapore to attend **LASALLE College of the Arts**, where she earned a degree in **Fashion Media and Industries**. Her training was originally in fashion and design, not acting — a background that distinguishes her from most of her contemporaries in Chinese entertainment.

She returned to China and entered the entertainment industry first through modeling and lifestyle media before transitioning to acting and singing.

---

## Acting Debut and Early Roles (2016–2019)

### *New Border Town Prodigal* (新边城浪子, 2016)

Yu Shuxin made her acting debut with a supporting role in *New Border Town Prodigal*, a wuxia drama adapted from a Gu Long novel. The role was small, but it gave her her first credited screen experience and entered her into the C-drama industry's casting circuits.

For several years afterward, she took supporting roles in costume and modern dramas while building visibility. Her early work tended toward lighter, more comedic roles — a register she returned to later when it suited the project.

---

## The Idol-Pop Pivot: *Youth With You* and THE9 (2020)

### *Youth With You* Season 2 (青春有你2)

In 2020, Yu Shuxin competed in the second season of **Youth With You**, a Chinese girl-group survival reality competition show. The competition was modeled on Korean idol-formation programs and ran across multiple weeks of public voting.

She placed high enough to debut as a member of the temporary girl group **THE9**, which formed on May 30, 2020. THE9 was a major commercial property in China for its active period — performing, releasing music, and operating as one of the most-followed girl groups of its generation.

Her time in THE9 dramatically expanded her audience. The reality-show breakout, combined with the group's commercial visibility, transitioned her from supporting-role actress to leading-role candidate. Her acting bookings post-THE9 reflected the shift.

---

## The Romantic-Lead Era: *Find Yourself* (2020)

### *Find Yourself* (下一站是幸福, 2020)

*Find Yourself* was Yu Shuxin's first significant supporting performance to attract critical attention. The show was a workplace romance in which she played a younger character against the central romantic couple. The role was not a lead, but it gave her a chance to develop the comedic timing and lighter-touch acting style that would characterize her romantic-lead roles to come — and it earned her a Golden Eagle audience nomination, an early signal of her growing public profile.

---

## The Breakout: *Love Between Fairy and Devil* (2022)

### Why It Mattered

**Love Between Fairy and Devil (苍兰诀)** is Yu Shuxin's career-defining role. The 2022 xianxia drama, in which she played the female lead **Xiao Lanhua** opposite Dylan Wang's Dongfang Qingcang, became one of the most-watched Chinese dramas of the year.

The show's success was not just commercial. It was a cultural moment. *Cang Lan Jue* trended on Weibo for months, generated billions of hashtag views on Douyin, and broke into international audiences via Viki and Netflix.

Yu Shuxin's performance — combining comedic timing with serious emotional range — was singled out as one of the central reasons the show worked. Critics noted that she handled the role's tonal shifts, from comic to tragic, with a level of skill that surprised viewers familiar only with her earlier romantic-lead work.

After *Love Between Fairy and Devil*, Yu Shuxin was no longer a rising actress. She was an established lead.

---

## The Established Lead: *My Journey to You* and *Love Game in Eastern Fantasy* (2023–2024)

### *My Journey to You* (云之羽, 2023)

A wuxia drama with a complex political-romance plot. Yu Shuxin played one of the female leads in an ensemble cast, working in the costume-action register that *Love Between Fairy and Devil* had established her in. The drama's reception was strong, and the show consolidated her position as a reliable wuxia and xianxia lead.

### *Love Game in Eastern Fantasy* (永夜星河, 2024)

A fantasy-romance hit pairing her with Ding Yuxi (丁禹兮). Playing a writer pulled into the world of her own story, Yu Shuxin leaned further into comedy than her previous costume leads, and the drama became one of the most-discussed of the year.

By this point, Yu Shuxin's catalog had developed a clear pattern: lead roles in xianxia, wuxia, and fantasy-romance productions, with occasional side ventures into modern material.

## The Career Shift: *Road to Success* (2026)

### Why This Project Is Different

**Road to Success (灿如繁星)**, scheduled for release on iQIYI in 2026, represents a deliberate shift from the costume and fantasy material Yu Shuxin built her career in.

She plays **Lin Wanxing (林晚星)**, a psychology PhD who returns to her hometown and takes a job as a sports equipment custodian at a high school. Her co-lead is **Chen Jingke** as a coach returning from a British professional club. The drama is a modern sports romance, adapted from Chang Er's (长洱) web novel **Narrow Road (狭路)**.

This is significant for several reasons:

1. **It's a contemporary setting.** Yu Shuxin's signature genre has been costume drama. Modern romance is a different audience and a different acting register.
2. **The character is older.** Lin Wanxing is a psychology PhD — late twenties, professionally educated, beyond the romantic-ingénue territory most of her costume roles occupied.
3. **The romance is workplace-driven, not chance-encounter-driven.** This is closer to a Western workplace-romance template than the typical C-drama love story.
4. **The stakes are educational and athletic, not magical.** No xianxia powers, no costume action — just a high school football team preparing for a championship while their coach and equipment manager develop a relationship.

For Yu Shuxin, the role represents a maturity statement. It says: I can carry a serious modern romance without the genre supports of fantasy and costume.

### What to Expect

Based on the source novel by Chang Er and the cast assembly, *Road to Success* is positioned as a modern romance for adult audiences rather than a youth-romance vehicle. The 36-episode arc culminates with the team's championship final paired with the gaokao (Chinese college entrance exam) — a Chinese cultural touchstone that signals the drama wants to talk about endurance, deferred reward, and the rewards of disciplined work.

## Yu Shuxin's Catalog: Best Dramas to Watch

For viewers discovering Yu Shuxin through *Road to Success*, here are her most essential earlier credits, ranked by impact:

1. **Love Between Fairy and Devil** (苍兰诀, 2022) — her career-defining xianxia drama. Essential viewing.
2. **My Journey to You** (云之羽, 2023) — strong ensemble wuxia.

4. **Love Game in Eastern Fantasy** (永夜星河, 2024) — recent fantasy-romance work.

For viewers more interested in her transition into modern realist material, *Road to Success* is the project to watch in 2026. It will tell us whether the modern-romance pivot becomes her new register or remains a single-project experiment.

## Music and Other Work

Beyond acting, Yu Shuxin has continued to release music, both as a solo artist and through her THE9 association. Her concerts and music videos carry significant audience attention in China, and she has built a brand identity that combines her acting work, fashion-design background, and idol-pop history.

This commercial breadth is part of why her career has remained durable. She is not dependent on any single category of work — actress, singer, fashion-adjacent personality — and the combination has insulated her career from the fortunes of any individual project.

## Why Yu Shuxin Has Mattered

Chinese drama has many actresses who break out and then plateau. Yu Shuxin's career has been characterized by deliberate range-expansion: from supporting comedic roles, to idol-pop debut, to xianxia lead, to modern realist drama. Each step has been a measurable expansion rather than a repetition of what worked before.

*Road to Success* is the next step. Whether it succeeds commercially or not, it represents the kind of risk-taking that has kept her career consistently visible across the past five years. Many of her contemporaries have stayed in the costume and fantasy lanes that made them famous. Yu Shuxin keeps reaching for material outside that comfort zone — and the cumulative effect is one of the more interesting careers in current Chinese drama.

For audiences new to her work, *Road to Success* is a clean entry point. For longtime fans, it is the latest chapter in a career that has consistently chosen ambition over repetition.
