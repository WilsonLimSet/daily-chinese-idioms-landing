**Pronunciation:** *jià qīng jiù shú*  
**Literal meaning:** Drive light vehicle, approach familiarity

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes driving (驾) a light (轻) vehicle and approaching (就) familiarity (熟). Similar to 轻车熟路, it depicts ease from experience. The phrase emphasizes how practice makes difficult things easy. Modern usage describes the ease that comes with mastery, handling complex tasks effortlessly because of extensive experience.

## Examples

**English:** "After years of practice, playing the piano came naturally."

**Chinese:** 经过多年练习，弹钢琴已驾轻就熟。
