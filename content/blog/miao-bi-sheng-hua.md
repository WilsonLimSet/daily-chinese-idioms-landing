**Pronunciation:** *miào bǐ shēng huā*  
**Literal meaning:** Wonderful brush produces flowers

## Origin & Usage

This idiom originates from the legend of the Tang Dynasty poet Li Bai, who allegedly dreamed that flowers bloomed from his writing brush, after which his poetry became extraordinarily beautiful. The 'wonderful brush' (妙笔) producing flowers (生花) symbolizes exceptional literary talent that brings words to life. The phrase celebrates the magical quality of excellent writing that transforms ordinary words into vivid imagery. Modern usage praises exceptional writing ability, eloquent expression, or creative skill in any artistic medium.

## Examples

**English:** "Her writing was so vivid that the scenes seemed to bloom from the page."

**Chinese:** 她的文笔妙笔生花，场景仿佛从纸上绽放。
