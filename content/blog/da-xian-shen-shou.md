**Pronunciation:** *dà xiǎn shēn shǒu*
**Literal meaning:** Greatly display one's abilities

## Origin & Usage

This idiom combines 'greatly display' (大显) with 'body and hands' (身手), where 'shenshou' represents one's physical abilities and skills. The phrase originated in martial arts contexts during the Ming Dynasty, describing warriors demonstrating their combat prowess. The theatrical element of 'display' suggests not just using abilities but showcasing them impressively. Modern usage extends to any context where someone has an opportunity to demonstrate their talents and skills, whether in sports, business, or creative endeavors.

## When to Use

**Situation:** The championship game was his chance to showcase his skills.
