**Pronunciation:** *yīn cái shī jiào*  
**Literal meaning:** Teach according to ability

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes teaching (施教) according to (因) ability (材). It embodies Confucius' educational philosophy of tailoring instruction to individual students' talents and needs. The phrase recognizes that different people learn differently. Modern usage praises adaptive teaching methods that recognize individual differences and adjust accordingly for optimal learning outcomes.

## Examples

**English:** "Good teachers adapt their methods to each student's learning style."

**Chinese:** 好老师会因材施教，根据每个学生的学习方式调整方法。
