**Pronunciation:** *gè xiǎn shén tōng*  
**Literal meaning:** Each displays divine powers

## Origin & Usage

This idiom originates from Buddhist and Daoist stories where supernatural beings each (各) displayed (显) their divine powers (神通). The phrase 'shentong' originally meant supernatural abilities of enlightened beings. It evolved to describe situations where different participants demonstrate their respective special skills or talents. Modern usage describes competitive or collaborative situations where individuals or teams showcase their unique capabilities and expertise.

## Examples

**English:** "In the competition, each team showcased their unique strengths."

**Chinese:** 比赛中，各队各显神通。
