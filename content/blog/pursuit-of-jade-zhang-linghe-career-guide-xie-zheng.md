## Who Is Zhang Linghe? — Short Answer

**Zhang Linghe (张凌赫, *Zhāng Línghè*) is the 28-year-old Chinese actor who plays Xie Zheng / Marquis Wu'an in Pursuit of Jade (逐玉, 2026)** — the role that made him one of China's top-tier 顶流 (top-traffic) stars and the first C-drama lead to chart on Netflix's weekly Global Top 10 Non-English TV list.

- **Born:** 30 December 1997, Wuxi, Jiangsu Province
- **Birth name:** Zhang Jiawei (张家玮)
- **Agency:** Star Times (宗星时代)
- **Background:** Studied electrical engineering at Nanjing Normal University before being scouted by a talent agent and pivoting to acting in 2020.
- **Breakout #1 (recognition):** Chang Heng / God of War in *Love Between Fairy and Devil* (苍兰诀, 2022) — the global Netflix hit headlined by Esther Yu and Dylan Wang where Zhang's "second-lead syndrome" performance launched his international fanbase.
- **Breakout #2 (A-list confirmation):** Xie Zheng in *Pursuit of Jade* (逐玉, 2026) — drew 3.3 billion+ aggregate views, ~750M weekly views in China, and made the Netflix global Top 10.
- **Western recognition:** Gucci brand ambassador since July 2024, plus Bulgari, Chopard, Roger Vivier, Lancôme. ~70,000+ fans on MyDramaList.

Below: his role in *Pursuit of Jade* explained, his full career arc, the "Foundation-Liquid General" meme that made the rounds on Weibo, and what's coming next.

## Who Plays Xie Zheng in Pursuit of Jade?

**Zhang Linghe plays Xie Zheng (谢征, *Xiè Zhēng*), the male lead of Pursuit of Jade** — opposite Tian Xiwei as Fan Changyu. He plays the character under two names through the drama, because Xie Zheng spends most of the early episodes hiding his identity:

- **Yan Zheng (言正)** — his alias, the name he uses when Fan Changyu first finds him half-dead in a snowstorm
- **Marquis of Wu'an (武安侯)** — his true noble title, revealed gradually across the season

### Xie Zheng's plot role

Xie Zheng is a young Marquis whose entire clan was massacred sixteen years earlier in a court conspiracy. He survives, gravely wounded, by being rescued from a snowstorm by **Fan Changyu** — a butcher's daughter from a remote village who needs a husband-of-convenience to keep her household intact. She proposes the contract marriage; he accepts, using her home as cover while he plans his revenge against the imperial court figures who destroyed his family.

The drama's structural premise — a fallen Marquis in disguise, a butcher's daughter who agrees to a 入赘 (matrilocal) marriage, the slow revelation of identity — is from the source novel *《逐玉》(Zhú Yù)* by **Tuan Zi Lai Xi (团子来袭)**. The contract-marriage-to-real-love arc is the engine of the whole season.

The strategy his character lives by is captured by a classical chengyu: ****韬光养晦**** (*tāo guāng yǎng huì*, "conceal the light, nurture in the dark") — Xie Zheng hides his title, his combat skill, and his lineage for over a decade, waiting until he has the position and the evidence to act. The chengyu **[卧薪尝胆](/blog/wo-xin-chang-dan)** (*wò xīn cháng dǎn*, "sleeping on firewood, tasting gall") names the same patient-revenge tradition.

For more on the character's finale arc — including how Fan Changyu becomes the Flower-Crowned General (簪花将军) — see [Does Fan Changyu Become a General? Pursuit of Jade Finale Explained](/blog/pursuit-of-jade-fan-changyu-finale-butcher-to-general).

## The Two-Stage Breakout: Chang Heng → Xie Zheng

Zhang Linghe's career has had **two distinct breakout moments** — and most Western fans know him from one but not the other.

### 2022: Chang Heng in Love Between Fairy and Devil (苍兰诀)

His first international moment came as the **second male lead** in *Love Between Fairy and Devil* — the global Netflix/Viki/WeTV hit headlined by Esther Yu (Yu Shuxin) and Dylan Wang. Zhang played **Chang Heng (长珩) / Xiao Run (萧润)**, the God of War of Shuiyuntian, in an unrequited-love arc that triggered the dreaded **"second-lead syndrome"** wave on Weibo, TikTok, and Twitter. International fandom adopted him then. This is also where his on-screen connection to **Esther Yu** began — he and Yu would co-lead *My Journey to You* (云之羽, 2023) a year later. See our [Esther Yu (Yu Shuxin) Career Guide](/blog/road-to-success-yu-shuxin-career-best-dramas-filmography) for that side of the story.

### 2026: Xie Zheng in Pursuit of Jade (逐玉)

*Pursuit of Jade* is the project that elevated him from popular costume-drama actor to **top-tier 顶流 (*dǐng liú*, "top-traffic")** star. By industry markers:

- **First mainland Chinese drama ever to chart on Netflix's weekly Global Top 10 Non-English TV** — debuted at #6, climbed to #5
- **~1.9 million Netflix views and 34.2 million hours watched in week 1**
- **~750 million weekly all-platform views in China** at peak
- **3.3 billion+ aggregate views** by April 2026 (a figure *Sixth Tone* has noted may be inflated; Chinese drama viewership claims are widely scrutinized)
- **14 subtitle languages, 5 dub tracks, 191 countries/regions**
- **#1 character-popularity ranking** for Zhang on Kuyun Data in week 11 of 2026

For SEO context: most "zhang linghe" English searches in 2026 originate from one of these two entry points — viewers who fell for Chang Heng in 2022 returning to look him up, or new Pursuit-of-Jade Netflix viewers searching him for the first time.

## The "粉底液将军" (Foundation-Liquid General) Meme

One unique angle in *Pursuit of Jade*'s reception that English-language coverage has largely missed: a half-mocking, half-affectionate Weibo meme that nicknamed Zhang's general-character **"粉底液将军"** (*fěn dǐ yè jiāng jūn*, "Foundation-Liquid General").

The joke: netizens noticed that Zhang's Xie Zheng character remains impeccably groomed even during battle scenes — his complexion stays flawless after bloodshed, as if he had reapplied foundation between takes. Critics on **Yicai** (the financial daily) and **Zhihu** argued that the high-gloss styling pulled viewers out of the drama's gritty premise. Fans defended the choice as part of the show's deliberate aestheticism.

The meme migrated to English-language fandom Twitter and TikTok as **"Foundation Liquid General"** and became a recurring half-affectionate fan reference. It captures a recurring tension around Zhang specifically: he is one of the most photogenic young leads in Chinese television, and his physical polish reads either as elegant or as miscast, depending on the role's tonal demands.

## What Makes Zhang Linghe's Trajectory Distinctive

Two patterns worth noting for anyone trying to understand where his career sits:

**The slow climb.** Zhang's first major role was 2020. The Chang Heng moment came two years later — and crucially, it was as a *second lead*. The Xie Zheng moment came another four years after that. Most current 顶流 stars cycle faster. The chengyu **[大器晚成](/blog/da-qi-wan-cheng)** (*dà qì wǎn chéng*, "a great vessel takes time to form") fits the shape of his ascent — recognition at 24, A-list confirmation at 28.

**The luxury-press signal.** His Gucci ambassadorship in mid-2024 was an early indicator that Western tastemakers were watching him before *Pursuit of Jade* broke globally. Western luxury houses pick brand faces with attention to drama trajectories they expect to land. The pattern usually correlates: where the Vogue / WWD profile pieces appear before the global drama hit, the global hit tends to follow.
