**Pronunciation:** *huàn dé huàn shī*  
**Literal meaning:** Worry about gain, worry about loss

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes worrying (患) about gaining (得) and worrying (患) about losing (失). It comes from Confucius' Analerta, criticizing petty people who are constantly anxious about what they might gain or lose. This mindset creates endless anxiety and prevents contentment. Modern usage describes excessive attachment to outcomes and the inability to find peace regardless of circumstances.

## Examples

**English:** "His constant anxiety about outcomes prevented him from enjoying the process."

**Chinese:** 他患得患失的心态让他无法享受过程。
