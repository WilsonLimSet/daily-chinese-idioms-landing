*Overdo* (这一秒过火) is set in **Republican-era Shanghai** — a world of foreign concessions, warlord families, and qipao-clad socialites. That setting makes it a rich source of period vocabulary. Here are the words worth knowing as you watch, with pinyin, meaning, and how they come up.

## The era and the city

**民国 (mín guó)** — the **Republic of China** era (1912–1949), the historical backdrop of the drama. "民国剧" (mínguó jù) is the whole genre of Republican-era dramas.

**上海滩 (Shànghǎi tān)** — literally "the Shanghai shore," this is *the* evocative name for old Shanghai, especially the glamorous, gangster-tinged Bund of the 1920s–30s.

**十里洋场 (shí lǐ yáng chǎng)** — "the ten-mile foreign zone," a classic nickname for cosmopolitan old Shanghai, packed with foreign concessions, dance halls, and money.

**旗袍 (qí páo)** — the **qipao** (cheongsam), the form-fitting dress that defines the era's fashion.

## People and titles

**少爷 (shào ye)** — "young master," the son of a wealthy or powerful family — like the male lead, Murong Qingyi.

**小姐 (xiǎo jiě)** — "young lady / miss," a respectful term for a woman of good family.

**太太 (tài tai)** — "madam / Mrs.," a married woman of status.

## Words for the plot

**抱错 (bào cuò)** — literally "held wrong," the term for a baby **switched at birth** — the wound at the center of Murong Qingyi's story.

**隐姓埋名 (yǐn xìng mái míng)** — a chengyu: "**hide your surname, bury your name**," i.e., to live in hiding under a false identity. It's exactly what Ren Susu does after faking her death.

**爱恨情仇 (ài hèn qíng chóu)** — "love, hate, passion, and revenge," the four-character shorthand for a melodrama like this one — the tangled emotions that drive the whole story.
