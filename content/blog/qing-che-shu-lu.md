**Pronunciation:** *qīng chē shú lù*  
**Literal meaning:** Light carriage, familiar road

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes a light (轻) carriage (车) on a familiar (熟) road (路). It depicts easy travel without burden on a well-known path. The phrase captures the ease of doing something familiar. Modern usage describes tasks one has done so often they require little effort, the comfort of experience making work easy.

## Examples

**English:** "Having done this many times, the process was easy for him."

**Chinese:** 做过很多次后，这个过程对他来说轻车熟路。
