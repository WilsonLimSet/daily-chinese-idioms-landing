**Pronunciation:** *huì rén bù juàn*  
**Literal meaning:** Teach others without weariness

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes teaching (诲) people (人) without (不) weariness (倦). It comes from the Analects where Confucius described his tireless dedication to education. The phrase embodies the ideal teacher who maintains enthusiasm despite repetition. Modern usage praises patient educators who never tire of helping others learn, maintaining dedication regardless of how many times they must explain.

## Examples

**English:** "Despite explaining it many times, she remained patient with the students."

**Chinese:** 尽管解释了很多遍，她仍然诲人不倦。
