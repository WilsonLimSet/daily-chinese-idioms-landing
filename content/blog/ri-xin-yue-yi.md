**Pronunciation:** *rì xīn yuè yì*  
**Literal meaning:** Day new month different

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes being new (新) every day (日) and different (异) every month (月). It captures rapid, continuous change and progress. The phrase echoes the Confucian classic 'Great Learning,' which advocates constant self-renewal. Originally about moral cultivation, it evolved to describe any field experiencing rapid development. Modern usage commonly describes technological advancement, economic growth, or any area of fast-paced change and improvement.

## Examples

**English:** "Technology is advancing at an unprecedented pace."

**Chinese:** 科技发展日新月异。
