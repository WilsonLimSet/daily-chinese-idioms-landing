**Pronunciation:** *bù xiè yī gù*
**Literal meaning:** Not deign one glance

## Origin & Usage

The 'Records of the Grand Historian' captures a haughty Jin Kingdom minister's refusal (不屑) to give even a single glance (一顾) at Wei's alliance proposal. Tang Dynasty writers embraced it as the ultimate expression of disdain, where even momentary attention is deemed too generous. Modern usage often carries a cautionary note about the dangers of such categorical dismissal.

## When to Use

**Situation:** The established professor dismissed the innovative theory without serious consideration
