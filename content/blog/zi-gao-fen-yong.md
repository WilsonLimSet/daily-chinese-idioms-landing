**Pronunciation:** *zì gào fèn yǒng*  
**Literal meaning:** Self-recommend with vigor and courage

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes volunteering (自告) with vigor (奋) and courage (勇), emphasizing initiative and bravery in stepping forward. The phrase combines self-nomination with the qualities needed to follow through. It appeared in historical texts describing brave soldiers or officials who volunteered for dangerous missions. The idiom values proactive contribution over passive waiting. Modern usage praises those who voluntarily take on challenges, especially difficult or risky ones that others avoid.

## Examples

**English:** "She volunteered to lead the difficult project."

**Chinese:** 她自告奋勇，主动承担这个困难的项目。
