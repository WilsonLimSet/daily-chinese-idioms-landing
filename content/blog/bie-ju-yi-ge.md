**Pronunciation:** *bié jù yī gé*  
**Literal meaning:** Separately possess one style

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes separately (别) possessing (具) one (一) style (格). It depicts something with a unique character that distinguishes it from others. The phrase values originality and distinctiveness. Modern usage describes anything with unique qualities that set it apart, whether artistic works, businesses, or personal styles.

## Examples

**English:** "The restaurant offers a distinctive dining experience."

**Chinese:** 这家餐厅提供别具一格的用餐体验。
