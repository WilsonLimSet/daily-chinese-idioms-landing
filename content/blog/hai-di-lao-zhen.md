**Pronunciation:** *hǎi dǐ lāo zhēn*
**Literal meaning:** Sea bottom dredge needle

## Origin & Usage

Ming Dynasty storytellers gave us this image of searching for a needle at the bottom of the ocean (海底捞针), originally appearing in tales of impossible tasks set for heroes. Qing Dynasty officials adopted it to describe particularly challenging investigations. The vast disparity between the search space and target size perfectly captures the frustration of seeking something minuscule in an overwhelming environment.

## When to Use

**Situation:** Finding the specific document among millions of unorganized files seemed impossible
