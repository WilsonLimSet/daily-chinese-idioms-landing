**Pronunciation:** *qià dào hǎo chù*  
**Literal meaning:** Exactly at the good place

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes being exactly (恰) at (到) the good (好) place (处). It depicts hitting the perfect point - neither too much nor too little. The phrase values precision and appropriateness. Modern usage describes things done with perfect calibration, actions or amounts that are exactly right for the situation.

## Examples

**English:** "The seasoning was perfect - not too much, not too little."

**Chinese:** 调味恰到好处，不多不少。
