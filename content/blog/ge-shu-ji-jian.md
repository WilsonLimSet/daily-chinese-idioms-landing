**Pronunciation:** *gè shū jǐ jiàn*
**Literal meaning:** Each express own view

## Origin & Usage

Spring and Autumn period diplomatic protocols established this principle of each person expressing (抒) their own (己) view (见). Warring States strategists refined it into a method for gathering diverse perspectives before making crucial decisions. The phrase emphasizes both the right and responsibility to contribute one's unique viewpoint to collective discussions.

## When to Use

**Situation:** The committee chair encouraged all members to share their perspectives on the proposal
