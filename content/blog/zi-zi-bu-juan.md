**Pronunciation:** *zī zī bù juàn*  
**Literal meaning:** Diligently, without fatigue

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes working diligently (孜孜) without (不) becoming weary (倦). The doubled character 孜 emphasizes continuous effort. The phrase praises sustained dedication that doesn't diminish over time. Modern usage describes tireless workers who maintain their diligence and enthusiasm despite long hours or years of effort, never losing their drive.

## Examples

**English:** "The researcher worked tirelessly on her project for years."

**Chinese:** 研究人员孜孜不倦地工作了多年。
