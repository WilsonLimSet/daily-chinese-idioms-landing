**Pronunciation:** *yī mù liǎo rán*  
**Literal meaning:** One look completely clear

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes becoming completely clear (了然) with one (一) look (目). It praises clarity that enables instant comprehension without explanation. The phrase appeared in texts praising well-organized information or transparent situations. It values effective communication and presentation. Modern usage describes anything designed for immediate understanding - from data visualizations to well-structured explanations to situations that are obviously clear.

## Examples

**English:** "The chart makes the data immediately understandable."

**Chinese:** 这张图表让数据一目了然。
