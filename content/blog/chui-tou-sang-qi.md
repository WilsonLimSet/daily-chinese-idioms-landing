**Pronunciation:** *chuí tóu sàng qì*  
**Literal meaning:** Drooping head, lost spirit

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes a drooping (垂) head (头) and lost (丧) spirit (气). It depicts the body language of defeat and discouragement - the slumped posture of someone who has given up hope. The phrase captures how disappointment manifests physically. Modern usage describes dejection, the visible loss of energy and hope following failure or bad news.

## Examples

**English:** "The losing team walked off the field dejected and dispirited."

**Chinese:** 输球的队伍垂头丧气地走下球场。
