**Pronunciation:** *àn bù jiù bān*  
**Literal meaning:** Follow departments, go to classes

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes following (按) departments (部) and going to (就) classes (班). Originally describing bureaucratic procedures, it came to mean following established order. The phrase values systematic approaches over improvisation. Modern usage describes following proper procedures and sequences, working methodically rather than chaotically, though sometimes criticizes excessive rigidity.

## Examples

**English:** "The project proceeded according to the established process."

**Chinese:** 项目按部就班地推进。
