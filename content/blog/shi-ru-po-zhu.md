**Pronunciation:** *shì rú pò zhú*  
**Literal meaning:** Momentum like splitting bamboo

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes momentum (势) like (如) splitting (破) bamboo (竹). When bamboo is split, the crack runs easily down the length. The phrase captures unstoppable momentum where each success makes the next easier. Modern usage describes winning streaks and momentum-driven success, situations where progress becomes increasingly easy as barriers fall.

## Examples

**English:** "Once they gained momentum, nothing could stop their advance."

**Chinese:** 一旦获得势头，他们的进攻势如破竹。
