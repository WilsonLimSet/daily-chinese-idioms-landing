**Pronunciation:** *rèn zhòng dào yuǎn*  
**Literal meaning:** Heavy responsibility, long road

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes heavy (重) responsibility (任) and a long (远) road (道). From the Analects where Confucius described the burden of benevolence. The phrase captures the weight of important duties that require long journeys. Modern usage describes significant undertakings that require sustained effort over time, acknowledging both the importance and difficulty of major goals.

## Examples

**English:** "Achieving sustainability is a long-term challenge requiring sustained effort."

**Chinese:** 实现可持续发展任重道远。
