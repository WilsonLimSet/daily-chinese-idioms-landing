**Pronunciation:** *fèi qǐn wàng shí*
**Literal meaning:** Abandon sleep forget food

## Origin & Usage

Han Dynasty biographers first celebrated this level of dedication where scholars abandoned sleep (废寝) and forgot food (忘食). The phrase gained immortality through Sima Qian's complete absorption in compiling the 'Records of the Grand Historian.' The neglect of basic biological needs demonstrated how intellectual passion could override even survival instincts.

## When to Use

**Situation:** The researcher became so absorbed in the project she barely ate or slept for days
