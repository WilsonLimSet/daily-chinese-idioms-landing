**Pronunciation:** *dé xīn yìng shǒu*  
**Literal meaning:** Heart obtains, hand responds

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes the heart (心) obtaining (得) and the hand (手) responding (应). It depicts perfect coordination between intention and execution. The phrase captures the experience of mastery where action flows naturally from thought. Modern usage describes performing tasks with complete facility, when skill has become so internalized that execution is effortless.

## Examples

**English:** "The artist's brush moved exactly as she imagined."

**Chinese:** 艺术家运笔得心应手。
