**Pronunciation:** *xīn xīn xiāng yìn*  
**Literal meaning:** Hearts imprint on each other

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes hearts (心心) imprinting (印) on each other (相). Originally a Buddhist term describing the transmission of dharma between master and disciple without words, it now primarily describes deep romantic connection. Modern usage depicts intimate understanding between lovers or close friends where communication transcends words - minds that think alike and hearts that beat as one.

## Examples

**English:** "The couple understood each other without words."

**Chinese:** 这对夫妻心心相印，不用言语就能理解对方。
