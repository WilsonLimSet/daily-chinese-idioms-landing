**Pronunciation:** *jiàn hǎo jiù shōu*  
**Literal meaning:** See good then collect

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes seeing (见) good conditions (好) and then (就) collecting/stopping (收). It advises knowing when to quit while ahead rather than pushing luck too far. The phrase embodies practical wisdom about limits. Modern usage advocates for taking gains when available, the prudence of securing success rather than risking it for more.

## Examples

**English:** "Knowing when to take profits is key in investing."

**Chinese:** 在投资中，见好就收是关键。
