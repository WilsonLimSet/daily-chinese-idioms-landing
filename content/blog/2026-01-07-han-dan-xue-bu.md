**Pronunciation:** *hán dān xué bù*
**Literal meaning:** Handan learn walking

## Origin & Usage

Zhuangzi's philosophical writings tell of a youth who so admired the walking style of Handan (邯郸) residents that in trying to learn it (学步), he forgot his natural gait and had to crawl home. Han Dynasty rhetoricians transformed this tale into a broader warning about the dangers of imitation. The story perfectly captures how pursuing artificial excellence can lead to losing natural competence.

## When to Use

**Situation:** The writer abandoned his natural style to poorly imitate a famous author
