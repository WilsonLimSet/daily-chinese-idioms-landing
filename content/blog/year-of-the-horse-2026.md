2026 is a **Year of the Horse (马, mǎ)** — the seventh sign of the Chinese zodiac and one of its most energetic. If you were born in a Horse year, or you want to know what a Horse year is supposed to bring, here is what the tradition says.

## When does the Year of the Horse 2026 begin?

The Chinese zodiac year does not start on January 1. It begins at **Chinese New Year**, which falls between late January and mid-February. So the 2026 Horse year starts at Chinese New Year 2026, not on New Year's Day — an important detail if your birthday is in January or early February, because you may still belong to the previous sign (the Snake). You can double-check with our [What Is My Chinese Zodiac Sign?](/zodiac/what-is-my-sign) tool.

Recent Years of the Horse include 2014, 2002, 1990, 1978, and 1966 — the sign returns every twelve years.

## The Horse personality

People born under the Horse are usually the ones who can't sit still. In Chinese culture the Horse (马) has long stood for movement and vitality — the animal that carried armies, messengers, and merchants across the empire — and that association shows up in Horse personalities as a love of travel, variety, and independence. A Horse tends to be warm, funny, and easy to like, throws itself wholeheartedly into whatever is new, and gets antsy the moment life turns repetitive. Its gift is momentum; its challenge is following through.

**Strengths:** energetic, independent, warm, adventurous, persuasive.
**Watch-outs:** restless, impatient, inconsistent, and sometimes blunt.

## Lucky numbers and colors for the Horse

By popular almanac tradition, the Horse is associated with the lucky **numbers 2, 3, and 7** and the lucky **colors yellow and green**. (These vary from source to source and are folklore, not a forecast.)

## Who is the Horse compatible with?

In Chinese zodiac tradition, the Horse belongs to a compatibility trine (三合) with the **Tiger** and the **Dog** — its most natural long-term matches. It also pairs well with the **Goat**, its "secret friend."

The Horse's most challenging match is the **Rat**, which sits directly opposite it on the zodiac wheel — the classic "six clash" (六冲). That does not doom the pairing, but it is the one that traditionally takes the most patience. See how every sign matches on our [compatibility chart](/zodiac/compatibility).

## What a Horse year is said to favor

A Horse year is traditionally seen as a time for movement, boldness, and fresh starts — travel, new ventures, and following an impulse you have been sitting on. The flip side of all that momentum is the Horse's classic pitfall: with so much energy in the air, it is easy to start ten things and finish none. The Horse's own lesson applies to its year — channel the drive, and see something through.
