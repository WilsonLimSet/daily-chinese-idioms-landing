**Pronunciation:** *guāng míng lěi luò*  
**Literal meaning:** Bright, clear, and upright

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes being bright (光明) and upright (磊落). The character 磊 shows three stones stacked, representing solidity and openness. The phrase depicts someone with nothing to hide, whose character is transparent and honorable. Modern usage describes people of integrity whose actions can withstand scrutiny, who act openly without hidden agendas.

## Examples

**English:** "His straightforward character earned him everyone's trust."

**Chinese:** 他光明磊落的性格赢得了大家的信任。
