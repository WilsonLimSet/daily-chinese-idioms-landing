**Pronunciation:** *yī yī bù shě*  
**Literal meaning:** Lingering reluctantly, unwilling to leave

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes lingering (依依) and being unwilling (不舍) to leave. The doubled 依 emphasizes the clinging, reluctant quality of the departure. The phrase captures the hesitation when leaving something beloved. Modern usage describes reluctant partings - children leaving fun places, lovers saying goodbye, or leaving any situation one wishes could continue.

## Examples

**English:** "The children reluctantly left the amusement park."

**Chinese:** 孩子们依依不舍地离开游乐园。
