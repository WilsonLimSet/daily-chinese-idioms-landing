**Pronunciation:** *qīng ér yì jǔ*  
**Literal meaning:** Light and easy to lift

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes something so light (轻) that it can be easily (易) lifted (举). The physical image of effortless lifting translates to any task accomplished without difficulty. The phrase appeared in classical texts praising skilled individuals for whom complex tasks seemed simple. It emphasizes the relationship between expertise and apparent ease. Modern usage describes tasks that are simple, either inherently or due to the skill of the person performing them, often used to express that something poses no challenge.

## Examples

**English:** "For an expert, solving this problem was effortless."

**Chinese:** 对于专家来说，解决这个问题轻而易举。
