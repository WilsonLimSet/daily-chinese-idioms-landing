**Pronunciation:** *jū gōng jìn cuì*  
**Literal meaning:** Bow down and exhaust oneself

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes bowing (鞠躬) and exhausting (尽) oneself (瘁). It comes from Zhuge Liang's famous memorial, describing his dedication to serving Shu until death. The phrase embodies the ultimate in loyal service. Modern usage describes complete dedication to a cause or organization, giving everything until nothing remains, the highest form of devoted service.

## Examples

**English:** "He served the organization until his final breath."

**Chinese:** 他鞠躬尽瘁，为组织服务到最后一刻。
