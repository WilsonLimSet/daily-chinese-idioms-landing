**Pronunciation:** *xún xún shàn yòu*  
**Literal meaning:** Patiently and skillfully guide

## Origin & Usage

This idiom describes guiding (循循) skillfully (善) and leading (诱). It comes from the Analects where Yan Hui praised Confucius' teaching method of systematic, patient guidance. The doubled 循 emphasizes the gradual, step-by-step approach. Modern usage describes excellent teaching methodology - patient, systematic instruction that leads students to understanding without force.

## Examples

**English:** "The mentor skillfully guided the students through difficult concepts."

**Chinese:** 导师循循善诱，带领学生理解困难的概念。
