## 1. What is one Chinese idiom or saying you frequently share with your children?

> Context: Real Quora question found by user. Answer directly and helpfully.

### Draft Answer:

One Chinese idiom I frequently share with my children is **风雨同舟** (fēng yǔ tóng zhōu), which translates to "share a boat in wind and rain." This saying emphasizes the importance of standing together through tough times, reminding them that family and friends should support each other during hardships.

I love this idiom because it teaches resilience and unity. For example, when my kids face challenges at school—be it a tough exam or a group project—I remind them that they’re all in it together, just like being in the same boat during a storm. 

Another idiom I often use is **守望相助** (shǒu wàng xiāng zhù), meaning "watch and help each other." This one reinforces the value of looking out for one another, whether it's helping a friend who’s feeling down or pitching in at home. 

Incorporating these idioms into our daily conversations not only enriches their language skills but also instills valuable life lessons.
