## Quote 1: "灵堂上的兄弟"

- **Chinese:** 他不是护卫，他是我兄弟。
- **Pinyin:** Tā bù shì hùwèi, tā shì wǒ xiōngdì.
- **English:** "He's not a bodyguard — he's my brother."
- **Speaker:** Fan Xian 范闲 (Zhang Ruoyun)
- **Scene:** Spoken in the funeral hall (灵堂) for Teng Zijing 滕梓荆, the loyal swordsman who dies protecting Fan Xian. When mourners and officials dismiss the dead man as "just a bodyguard," Fan Xian explodes.
- **Cultural / literary context:** This line is the structural heir to Ye Qingmei's monument inscription "人人生而平等" ("all are born equal") that Fan Xian reads later. It collapses the Qing-dynasty class hierarchy into one sentence: a hired swordsman is rhetorically equal to a noble son.
- **Why it matters:** Fans across Tencent News, 163.com and QQ News commentary cite this as Fan Xian's "moral pivot" of Season 2 — the moment he stops mediating his idealism through wit and starts confronting power directly.
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 小人物之死震醒范闲](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20251203A0324E00); [163 — 庆余年2：堪称封神的4句台词](https://c.m.163.com/news/a/J3SDTBJO0528DKU1.html)

## Quote 2: "甘愿赴死的人"

- **Chinese:** 这世上若没有让你甘愿赴死的人，活着何其无趣。
- **Pinyin:** Zhè shì shàng ruò méi yǒu ràng nǐ gānyuàn fùsǐ de rén, huózhe hé qí wúqù.
- **English:** "If there is no one in this world worth dying for, what joy is there in being alive?"
- **Speaker:** Teng Zijing 滕梓荆 (Zhao Wenhao) to Fan Xian
- **Scene:** Early-season exchange when Teng Zijing affirms his loyalty to Fan Xian — foreshadowing his death soon after.
- **Cultural / literary context:** A modern construction in the register of 士为知己者死 ("a shi-class warrior dies for one who knows him") from Sima Qian's *Records of the Grand Historian / 史记·刺客列传*. Wang Juan reworks the classical sentiment into colloquial Mandarin so it doesn't sound like a costume drama cliché.
- **Why it matters:** Listed by 163.com as one of the "four god-tier lines" (堪称封神的4句台词) of S2. It became a widely shared Weibo card with calligraphy overlays after Teng Zijing's death episode.
- **Sources:** [163 — 庆余年2：堪称封神的4句台词](https://c.m.163.com/news/a/J3SDTBJO0528DKU1.html); [QQ News — 最火的7句台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240601A08IJX00)

## Quote 3: "爹例外"

- **Chinese:** 这世上没有人天生应该对你好，爹例外。
- **Pinyin:** Zhè shì shàng méi yǒu rén tiānshēng yīnggāi duì nǐ hǎo, diē lìwài.
- **English:** "No one in this world is born owing you kindness — your father excepted."
- **Speaker:** Prime Minister Lin Ruofu 林若甫 (Yu Yang 于洋) to his elder son Da-bao 大宝
- **Scene:** Episode 23. Lin Ruofu has been forced into retirement after clashing with the Emperor. Knowing the Emperor will likely send killers after him, he says goodbye to his intellectually-disabled son Da-bao and entrusts him to Fan Xian and Wan'er.
- **Cultural / literary context:** A grammatically classical construction (the "Y 例外" carve-out) deployed for maximum emotional sting. It inverts the standard Confucian premise that filial relationships are *obligatory*, recasting paternal love as a gift rather than a duty.
- **Why it matters:** Multiple aggregators (Tencent News, Sohu, 163, 360 Entertainment) flagged this as *the* most-shared S2 line on Weibo. Listed first in the QQ News "Top 7 most viral lines" roundup published the day after the finale.
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 最火的7句台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240601A08IJX00); [163 — 堪称封神的4句台词](https://c.m.163.com/news/a/J3SDTBJO0528DKU1.html); [QQ News — 拍案叫绝的经典台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240605A0AM5400)

## Quote 4: "御史风骨"

- **Chinese:** 身为御史，就该有连陛下也敢参的气魄。
- **Pinyin:** Shēn wéi yùshǐ, jiù gāi yǒu lián bìxià yě gǎn cān de qìpò.
- **English:** "As a censor (yushi), one must possess the courage to impeach even the Emperor himself."
- **Speaker:** Lai Mingcheng 赖名成 (Bi Yanjun 毕彦君), Censor-in-Chief
- **Scene:** Court audience in the middle of the season, immediately before his death by 杖毙 (beating to death on the Emperor's order).
- **Cultural / literary context:** This is the textbook *yushi* ideal codified since the Tang. The line that follows on the same arc — 人非圣贤，孰能无过 ("no one is a sage; who is without fault?") — directly quotes the *Zuo Zhuan / 左传·宣公二年*, a Spring-and-Autumn-era classic. Lai Mingcheng's "我以我血荐轩辕" allusion (sources note this phrasing was raised in commentary, though not spoken in dialogue) borrows from Lu Xun's 1903 poem *自题小像* — "I pledge my blood to the Yellow Emperor" — making him a modern intellectual-martyr cosplaying inside a costume drama.
- **Why it matters:** Lai Mingcheng's death scene (the "杖毙御史" beat) is widely cited as the second emotional peak of the season after Lin Ruofu's farewell. The line trended in the QQ "top 7" roundup and was cited by procuratorate-affiliated commentary as a contemporary anti-corruption metaphor (see Fuyang prosecutor's office viewing-essay).
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 最火的7句台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240601A08IJX00); [Fuyang Prosecutor's Office — 观《庆余年2》有感](https://www.fyjcy.gov.cn/articleView.do?art_id=1584); [360 Yule — 拍案叫绝的经典台词](https://m.yule.360.com/content/3732731)

## Quote 5: "以血引雷霆"

- **Chinese:** 世间多不公，以血引雷霆。
- **Pinyin:** Shìjiān duō bùgōng, yǐ xuè yǐn léitíng.
- **English (literal):** "This world is full of injustice — summon thunder with blood."
- **English (nuanced):** "When the world is rotten with injustice, blood is the only call that wakes the heavens."
- **Speaker:** Deng Ziyue 邓子越 (Yu Ailei 余皑磊), a long-compromised Inspectorate clerk
- **Scene:** Episode 9. Director Sun Hao publicly named the Deng-Ziyue–Fan-Xian confrontation as his single favorite scene in the entire season. Deng, who long ago smuggled corruption evidence out of a frontier army at great personal cost but has since shrunk into careful mediocrity, finally articulates the conviction he buried.
- **Cultural / literary context:** A wholly modern construction by Wang Juan, in the cadence of classical four-character parallelism. 雷霆 ("thunder") echoes 雷霆之怒 / 雷霆之威, the imperial-wrath idiom, but here it's the people's wrath summoned from below.
- **Why it matters:** Yu Ailei's ten-minute performance became one of S2's most-clipped moments on Bilibili. Bilibili compilation channels use the line as the title of their S2 supporting-cast video essays. Listed in 163's "four god-tier lines."
- **Sources:** [Bilibili compilation — "世间多不公，以血引雷霆"](https://www.bilibili.com/video/BV1q4QuB7EuW/); [Douban movie review — 那些熠熠生辉的配角团](https://m.douban.com/movie/review/15967556/); [163 — 堪称封神的4句台词](https://c.m.163.com/news/a/J3SDTBJO0528DKU1.html); [Sohu — 邓子越一场戏让观众泪目](https://www.sohu.com/a/780521364_476413)

## Quote 6: "我是你的靠山"

- **Chinese:** 我是你的靠山。
- **Pinyin:** Wǒ shì nǐ de kàoshān.
- **English:** "I am your backing." / "I'll be the mountain you can lean on."
- **Speaker:** Fan Xian 范闲 to Deng Ziyue
- **Scene:** The other half of the Episode 9 confrontation — Fan Xian's reply that re-lights the older man's idealism.
- **Cultural / literary context:** 靠山 ("a mountain to lean on") is the standard Chinese metaphor for a powerful patron. In Qing-era court politics this was usually transactional. Fan Xian inverts the meaning: not "I'll protect you because you serve me" but "I'll protect you because you are right."
- **Why it matters:** Frequently paired with Deng Ziyue's "以血引雷霆" as call-and-response in fan edits. Cited in the QQ News "拍案叫绝" feature.
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 拍案叫绝的经典台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240605A0AM5400); [360 Yule — 邓子越对峙](https://yule.360.com/detail/3721443)

## Quote 7: "席总会散，人总要分"

- **Chinese:** 席总会散，人总要分，别送了！
- **Pinyin:** Xí zǒng huì sàn, rén zǒng yào fēn, bié sòng le!
- **English:** "Every banquet must end, every gathering must part — don't see me off!"
- **Speaker:** Fei Jie 费介 (Li Jian 李建义), Fan Xian's poison-craft master, to Fan Xian
- **Scene:** Their first parting in S2 — Fei Jie leaving the capital, leading his horse away.
- **Cultural / literary context:** The cadence echoes Tang and Song farewell verse. The "天下无不散之筵席" ("no banquet under heaven that does not end") proverb is canonical, attested in *红楼梦 / Dream of the Red Chamber* Chapter 26. Fei Jie compresses the proverb into spoken form and tacks on the irritated "别送了" — making a master-disciple parting feel like an old man shooing off a fly.
- **Why it matters:** Listed in the QQ "Top 7" as a viral S2 line. Resonated heavily during the season's run because Lin Ruofu's parting comes shortly after.
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 最火的7句台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240601A08IJX00); [Zhihu — 庆余年2台词讨论](https://www.zhihu.com/question/656390939)

## Quote 8: "迷雾里寻不见人，把自己化为灯塔"

- **Chinese:** 迷雾里寻不见人，那就把我们自己化为灯塔。
- **Pinyin:** Míwù lǐ xún bù jiàn rén, nà jiù bǎ wǒmen zìjǐ huà wéi dēngtǎ.
- **English:** "If you can find no one through the fog, then become a lighthouse yourself."
- **Speaker:** Narrative voice / Fan Xian (used as season-thematic line in promotional materials and one of his interior monologues)
- **Scene:** Recurs across S2 as Fan Xian's animating philosophy; framed by the show's title sequence and end-of-season recap.
- **Cultural / literary context:** A modern lyrical construction — 灯塔 (lighthouse) is not a classical Chinese image but a 20th-century import. The line consciously breaks with the classical register, marking Fan Xian as the "modern soul" trapped in a Qing-style empire (mirroring how Ye Qingmei is treated in the source novel).
- **Why it matters:** Quoted by Tencent News and 163.com as the season's thematic spine. Used as headline material in multiple S2 retrospectives.
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 最火的7句台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240601A08IJX00); [163 — 最火台词，每一句都是名场面](https://c.m.163.com/news/a/J6OHBJTC05565JHS.html); [Zhihu zhuanlan — 经典高燃语录](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/705850087)

## Quote 9: "不羁之民"

- **Chinese:** 我希望庆国的人民都能成为不羁之民。受到他人虐待时有不屈服之心，受到灾恶侵袭时有不受挫折之心，受到束缚压迫时有不受捆绑之心，受到歪曲与不公时有不受冤屈之心。
- **Pinyin:** Wǒ xīwàng Qìngguó de rénmín dōu néng chéngwéi bùjī zhī mín. Shòudào tārén nüèdài shí yǒu bù qūfú zhī xīn, shòudào zāi'è qīnxí shí yǒu bù shòu cuòzhé zhī xīn, shòudào shùfù yāpò shí yǒu bù shòu kǔnbǎng zhī xīn, shòudào wāiqū yǔ bùgōng shí yǒu bù shòu yuānqū zhī xīn.
- **English:** "I hope every citizen of Qing can become an unfettered people. When abused, may they not yield; when struck by calamity, may they not break; when bound and oppressed, may they not feel chained; when distorted and wronged, may they not feel aggrieved."
- **Speaker:** Originally Ye Qingmei 叶轻眉 (Fan Xian's mother), engraved on the stele outside the Inspectorate (鉴查院). Re-read aloud by Fan Xian in S2 as his political credo.
- **Scene:** Multiple recurrences across the season — including the stele scene and Fan Xian's reflection on his mother's legacy.
- **Cultural / literary context:** Ye Qingmei is a modern woman transmigrated into the world. The inscription consciously echoes the rhetoric of modern Chinese constitutionalism and Enlightenment-era rights language ("unfettered citizens" / "every citizen a king"), grafted onto a Qing-style courtly setting. The Fuyang prosecutor's office cites the further line "无越法之人、无不平之事、无忘本之心" ("no one breaks the law, no injustice goes unaddressed, no one forgets their roots") in a public-prosecution viewing essay.
- **Why it matters:** This is the show's *manifesto* — the through-line connecting S1 and S2. State-affiliated outlets (Xinhua, People's Daily local bureaus, Fuyang prosecutorial office) cited it to position the drama as a vehicle for rule-of-law messaging.
- **Sources:** [Baidu Baike — 叶轻眉](https://baike.baidu.com/item/%E5%8F%B6%E8%BD%BB%E7%9C%89/3097199); [Fuyang Prosecutor's Office](https://www.fyjcy.gov.cn/articleView.do?art_id=1584); [Zhihu — 庆余年深度解析](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/96842182)

## Quote 10: "礼法不认，我认"

- **Chinese:** 礼法不认，我认。
- **Pinyin:** Lǐfǎ bù rèn, wǒ rèn.
- **English:** "The rites of law don't recognize it — I do."
- **Speaker:** Fan Xian 范闲 to Aunt Liu (柳如玉 Liu Ruyu, his stepmother)
- **Scene:** Fan Xian's wedding to Lin Wan'er. By Qing-style 礼法 (rites and law), Liu Ruyu — as a concubine, not the principal wife — cannot occupy the matriarch's seat to receive the couple's bows. Fan Xian publicly overrides convention.
- **Cultural / literary context:** 礼法 in classical thought refers to the binding compound of *li* (Confucian ritual propriety) and *fa* (legal code). Refusing 礼法 in such a public ceremony — and asserting personal recognition as the new authority — is a small revolutionary gesture inside a costume drama. The construction "X 不认，我认" became a viral template on social media after the episode aired.
- **Why it matters:** Listed in QQ News's "拍案叫绝" feature. Wedding-episode highlight reels on Douyin and Bilibili lead with this beat.
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 拍案叫绝的经典台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240605A0AM5400); [Zhihu zhuanlan — 庆余年2名场面盘点](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/700393446)

## Quote 11: "退路与绝路"

- **Chinese:** 做人可不能瞻前顾后，想给自己留退路的，往往都成了绝路。
- **Pinyin:** Zuò rén kě bù néng zhānqián-gùhòu, xiǎng gěi zìjǐ liú tuìlù de, wǎngwǎng dōu chéng le juélù.
- **English:** "Don't hesitate looking forward and back — those who try to leave themselves an escape route usually end up at a dead end."
- **Speaker:** Second Prince Li Chengze 李承泽 (Liu Duanduan 刘端端)
- **Scene:** Spoken in S2 by the doomed Second Prince — a man who has spent the entire show trying (and failing) to leave himself escape routes.
- **Cultural / literary context:** 瞻前顾后 ("looking ahead and behind") is a classical four-character idiom from *屈原·离骚* (Qu Yuan's "Li Sao," ~3rd c. BCE), originally describing prudence; here Li Chengze inverts it into a vice. The pairing of 退路 / 绝路 ("retreat path" / "dead-end path") is a stock rhetorical antithesis the prince delivers with characteristic theatricality.
- **Why it matters:** Liu Duanduan's portrayal of the Second Prince — flamboyant, self-aware, doomed — was one of S2's fan-favorite arcs. This line is cited by QQ News and 163 as one of his signature lines and was widely screen-capped after his death scene.
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 拍案叫绝的经典台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240605A0AM5400); [Zhihu — 庆余年2台词讨论](https://www.zhihu.com/question/656390939)

## Quote 12: "拥有一切的人，终究一无所有"

- **Chinese:** 陛下，臣妾听说，拥有一切的人，终究一无所有。
- **Pinyin:** Bìxià, chénqiè tīng shuō, yǒngyǒu yīqiè de rén, zhōngjiù yīwúsuǒyǒu.
- **English:** "Your Majesty, I have heard it said — those who possess everything, in the end, possess nothing."
- **Speaker:** The Empress 皇后 (Yu Feihong 俞飞鸿)
- **Scene:** Near the end of the season, in the Empress's last meaningful exchange with the Qing Emperor 庆帝 before her downfall.
- **Cultural / literary context:** Echoes Daoist paradox — 老子·《道德经》 chapter 22: "曲则全……少则得，多则惑" ("Yielding becomes whole; what is reduced is gained, what is multiplied is confused") and chapter 44's 知足不辱 ("contentment averts shame"). Yu Feihong delivers it as if quoting an aphorism — making it sound older and heavier than it is.
- **Why it matters:** Cited by QQ News's "拍案叫绝" feature as one of the most chilling lines of the season — a quiet prophecy delivered to the all-powerful Emperor by the woman he has spent two seasons grinding down. Widely clipped after the finale aired.
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 拍案叫绝的经典台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240605A0AM5400)

## Quote 13: "李云睿最美"

- **Chinese:** 我这辈子，有一点是认准的。这天上地下，我李云睿最美。虽然有人眼瞎看不见我，但也不是我的错。
- **Pinyin:** Wǒ zhè bèizi, yǒu yī diǎn shì rèn zhǔn de. Zhè tiānshàng dìxià, wǒ Lǐ Yúnruì zuì měi. Suīrán yǒu rén yǎn xiā kàn bù jiàn wǒ, dàn yě bù shì wǒ de cuò.
- **English:** "There is one thing I've decided this lifetime: in heaven above and on earth below, I, Li Yunrui, am the most beautiful. Some people are blind and can't see me — but that isn't my fault."
- **Speaker:** Princess Li Yunrui 长公主李云睿 (Li Xiaoran 李小冉)
- **Scene:** Episode 25. Sneaking into the capital against the Emperor's order, she does her daughter Wan'er's hair on the eve of Wan'er's wedding — staring at her own reflection.
- **Cultural / literary context:** 天上地下 ("above heaven, below earth") is the Buddhist superlative famously attached to the infant Buddha's pronouncement 天上地下，唯我独尊. Li Yunrui appropriates Buddhist cosmology to crown her own vanity. Mainland critics called the line "比疯还美" ("more beautiful than her madness").
- **Why it matters:** Li Xiaoran's "mad princess" performance was one of S2's defining supporting turns. The line became a "self-confidence" meme template on Douyin and Xiaohongshu. Cited specifically in the QQ News "拍案叫绝" line-up as her signature.
- **Sources:** [QQ News — 拍案叫绝的经典台词](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240605A0AM5400); [QQ News — 天下第一美人李云睿](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240601A05SVJ00); [163 — 长公主下班](https://m.163.com/dy/article_cambrian/J3O4VPTQ055633I6.html)

## Quote 14: "传火的人"

- **Chinese:**
  陈萍萍：千里万里奔赴京都，春闱对他们来说，不光是一次考试，更像是一种希望。
  范闲：我娘活着的时候，想要彻底改变这个世界。
  陈萍萍：不光是改变世界，你娘还说要改变的还有人心。
  范闲：没想到啊，有一天我会当传火的人。
- **Pinyin (Fan Xian's line):** Méi xiǎng dào a, yǒu yī tiān wǒ huì dāng chuán huǒ de rén.
- **English:** "I never imagined — that one day I would be the one carrying the torch."
- **Speakers:** Chen Pingping 陈萍萍 (Wu Gang 吴刚) and Fan Xian, in conversation about the spring imperial exam (春闱).
- **Scene:** Fan Xian, having decided to crush the rigged exam, reflects with Chen Pingping on his late mother's wider ambition — to change not only the world but human hearts.
- **Cultural / literary context:** 传火 / "carrying the torch / fire" is a charged image — Lu Xun famously translated *prométhée* as 普罗米修斯, and the "torchbearer" trope (盗火者) is a 20th-century Chinese-intellectual self-image (the one who steals fire for the people). The series literalizes that metaphor with Ye Qingmei's modern ideals being relayed forward.
- **Why it matters:** Marks Fan Xian's explicit acceptance of his mother's mission. Quoted in Sun Hao / Wang Juan interviews and in retrospectives at chasingdramas.com, ThePaper, and 360 Yule.
- **Sources:** [ThePaper — 主创谈《庆余年2》](https://www.thepaper.cn/newsDetail_forward_27669614); [Zhihu — 庆余年2台词讨论](https://www.zhihu.com/question/656390939); [Xinhua — 燃爽内核下的群像成长](http://www.news.cn/ent/20240527/7ac1b90cc3ab4059a8dd002c1839b3a8/c.html)

## Quote 15: "自以为是的好人"

- **Chinese:** 我不是我娘，做不到为了改变这个世界而活着，但我活这一世，最起码想要做一个自以为是的好人，让我的良心能平静一些。
- **Pinyin:** Wǒ bù shì wǒ niáng, zuò bù dào wèile gǎibiàn zhège shìjiè ér huózhe, dàn wǒ huó zhè yī shì, zuì qǐmǎ xiǎng yào zuò yī gè zì yǐ wéi shì de hǎo rén, ràng wǒ de liángxīn néng píngjìng yīxiē.
- **English:** "I am not my mother. I can't live to change the world. But living this one life, the least I want is to be a self-righteous good man — at least let my conscience rest a little easier."
- **Speaker:** Fan Xian 范闲 (interior monologue / dialogue with Fan Ruoruo 范若若)
- **Scene:** Mid-to-late season, after Fan Xian recognizes that personal vengeance does not move the structural rot of the Qing court.
- **Cultural / literary context:** 自以为是 ("self-righteous / self-assured") is normally a negative classical idiom (from *孟子·尽心下* — Mencius — where 自以为是 describes the "village worthy" hypocrite). Fan Xian deliberately reclaims the slur as an ironic self-description: I'd rather be the self-righteous fool than the cynic who sleeps well.
- **Why it matters:** Cited by 163 and Xinjiang Mingtian (石榴云) in S2 think-pieces as the most honest articulation of the season's central tension — idealism downsized to the personal level. The reclaiming of 自以为是 is a Wang-Juan signature linguistic move.
- **Sources:** [163 — 庆余年2的爽点](https://www.163.com/dy/article/J2IBACN50528N3L3.html); [Xinjiang Mingtian — 5条处世哲学](https://www.xjmty.com/baodi/202405/t20240526_21522706_web.html)

## Verification Flags

Quotes / claims I encountered but could **not** confirm to a level of confidence sufficient for direct attribution:

1. **"权力是一把双刃剑，握之则伤，弃之则弱"** ("Power is a double-edged sword — to hold it wounds you, to drop it weakens you") — frequently attributed online to Fan Xian, but the same aggregator (360 Yule "高考语文高分秘籍" piece) appears to mix S1 and S2 quotes loosely. I could not find a single dialogue-context citation that places it in S2 specifically. **Treat as unverified.**

2. **"人生如逆旅，我亦是行人"** — This is verbatim 苏轼 / Su Shi's *《临江仙·送钱穆父》* (Song dynasty). It is *quoted by* fans as something Fan Xian "would say" but I could not confirm Fan Xian speaks this verbatim in S2 (vs. being used as a thematic gloss in promotional materials). **Verify against episode footage before quoting in article.**

3. **"世间万物，皆有定时"** — Echoes Ecclesiastes 3:1 ("a time for everything") and similar wisdom-literature constructions. Listed in some aggregators as Fan Xian's line but cross-referencing showed these summaries tend to blur S1, S2 and novel quotes together. **Unverified for S2 specifically.**

4. **"将人心做战场，才是最惊心动魄的"** — Appeared once in a 360 Yule aggregation as an "S2 important line." Could not find a second source. **Unverified.**

5. **"有我，你就有家" / "今晚给你的必须完美"** — Listed in the QQ News "拍案叫绝" piece as Fan Xian lines (to Chen Pingping and to Wan'er respectively). They are confirmed by *that* aggregator but I did not find a second source corroborating the exact wording. **Single-source — use with caution.**

6. **The line "我以我血荐轩辕"** is associated in commentary with Lai Mingcheng's *spirit*, drawn from Lu Xun's 1903 poem *自题小像*, but I did not find a citation that Lai Mingcheng speaks this verbatim in-episode. **Treat as commentary framing, not in-script dialogue.**

7. **"愿所有忍耐蛰伏都遇惊雷，愿一切磨难困苦不扰心湖"** — Quoted by a single aggregator (QQ News) as a finale-episode line; phrasing is more in the register of a viewer-card / closing-narration than dialogue. **Possibly card-text rather than spoken line — flag for the writer to confirm against episode footage.**

8. **Episode-number assignments**: I have flagged episode numbers (e.g., Quote 3 "Episode 23," Quote 5 "Episode 9," Quote 13 "Episode 25") *as reported by aggregators*. These should be re-verified against the show's actual episode breakdown if any episode citation goes into the published article. Single-source episode numbers should be softened to "mid-season" / "late-season" in the published piece unless re-confirmed.
