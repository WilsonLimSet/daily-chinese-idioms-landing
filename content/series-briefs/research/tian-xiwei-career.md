## Role in *Pursuit of Jade* (逐玉)

Tian Xiwei plays **Fan Changyu (范长玉)**, the female lead of the 40-episode 2026 historical romance *Pursuit of Jade* (逐玉, *Zhú Yù*), which premiered worldwide on 6 March 2026 on Tencent Video, iQIYI, Netflix, and Dragon TV. Her co-lead is Zhang Linghe as Xie Zheng / Yan Zheng.

### Character core

- **Origin**: A butcher's daughter from a remote village. Orphaned after her parents were murdered by bandits; she works as a butcher to raise her younger sister Ningning.
- **Defining trait**: "Feisty and resilient" with notable physical strength — she wields a butcher's knife as both her tool and her weapon. Her thesis line, much-quoted by fans: *"I am a butcher's daughter; with my knife I can slaughter pigs, and I can also protect mountains and rivers."*
- **入赘 (matrilocal / "groom marries in") marriage premise**: Changyu's neighbors urge her to take a 入赘 husband so her uncle can't seize her family's house. Xie Zheng — fallen Marquis of Wu'an, severely wounded and in hiding — agrees to marry *into* her family rather than the reverse. Wikipedia explicitly notes that even after his identity as Marquis Wu'an is revealed, "he will always be her matrilocal husband."
- **Arc**: From village butcher → wartime defender of her home → battlefield commander searching for her husband → ennobled as **General Huaihua (怀化将军)** → ends the drama as Grand General and First-Rank Lady Protector of the State, governing alongside Xie Zheng as Prince Regent.
- **Standout scene driving fandom virality**: Episodes 18–19, the gua sha (刮痧) scene where Xie Zheng treats her injuries, was one of the most-active episode discussion threads in r/CDrama history per fandom recaps. The villain kill — Changyu using her father's butcher knife on the antagonist — anchors her finale.

### Couple-name with Zhang Linghe

The fandom ship name is **"Zhengyu" (征玉)** — a homophone play on Xie *Zheng* + Fan Chang*yu*, with 征 ("conquest/expedition") swapped in for stylistic punch. The pairing has been heavily marketed; their managers (per fan accounts) coordinated a "historic reconciliation" of their fanbases after earlier hostility. Real-life dating rumors broke on 6 April 2026 after the two were spotted at a Hengdian cafe in matching hats and masks — neither has confirmed, and earlier rumors (Qu Chuxiao 2022, Lai Weiming 2024) were denied.

### Performance reception

Western and pan-Asian reviewers have praised Tian's performance as "grounded and quietly commanding," a refreshing pivot away from the fragile-heroine archetype. *Pursuit of Jade* posted a Tencent heat index above 31,000 and an iQIYI index above 10,000 in week one, total views above 3.6 billion, and merchandise sales above 20M RMB — a commercial peak for both leads.

## Breakout Role

**New Life Begins (卿卿日常, 2022)** as Li Wei.

This is the consensus breakout. The romantic-comedy-meets-political-marriage premise opposite Bai Jingting became the fastest iQIYI drama to hit a popularity heat index of 10,000 at the time, with a 19.6 peak rating and 1.26M peak viewership in week one. It established Tian's "round-faced sweet girl" screen image, gave her national name recognition, and triggered her first major brand deals.

**Subsequent consolidation**: *Wrong Carriage, Right Groom* (2023) and *Guardians of the Dafeng* (2024, as Princess Lin'an opposite Dylan Wang) confirmed she could sustain ratings. *Guardians of the Dafeng* pushed past 30,000 Tencent heat — a tier almost no actress in her cohort had hit.

**Career-peak role**: *Pursuit of Jade* (2026) is the one that flipped her from "popular young dan with hot dramas" to "carrying a flagship Netflix/iQIYI co-production with global Top 10 exposure." The producer Yu Zheng publicly placed her in the "four small dan" rising tier (alongside Zhou Ye, Zhang Jingyi, and Wang Churan), one rung below the "four big dan" (Yang Zi, Bai Lu, Zhao Lusi, Esther Yu) — *Pursuit of Jade* is widely read as her promotion case to that top tier.

## Community Discussion Themes (r/CDrama, MDL, X-Twitter, Zhihu)

- **Gua sha scene (Eps 18-19) discourse**: Most-replied *Pursuit of Jade* episode thread on r/CDrama. Fan debate centered on intimacy framing and whether the scene crossed mainland broadcast norms.
- **Chemistry vs. acting separation**: Reviewers describe the Zhang Linghe/Tian Xiwei chemistry as "dangerously convincing." A common framing: the chemistry is the show's primary engine, more so than the political plotting.
- **"Fake sweet" criticism (legacy)**: Earlier in her career — primarily 2022-2024 — Tian was tagged by some Weibo/Zhihu commenters as "fake sweet" (假甜), a critique aimed at her round-faced, dimpled image being read as performative. *Pursuit of Jade* has substantially blunted that criticism because the role demanded toughness and physicality.
- **Compared to the "four big dan"**: The Yu Zheng tier list explicitly places her in the rising "four small dan" (along with Zhou Ye, Zhang Jingyi, Wang Churan) — one notch below Yang Zi, Bai Lu, Zhao Lusi, Esther Yu. Fan debate: will *Pursuit of Jade* be her promotion case, similar to how *The Romance of Tiger and Rose* elevated Zhao Lusi?
- **"Dream goddess" / "first love goddess" tag**: Post-*Pursuit of Jade*, fan content has labeled her "China's first love goddess" and one YouTube short claims she's "surpassed Liu Yifei as the new dream goddess" — marketing framing, not consensus, but indicative of the brand tier she's being positioned for.
- **Comparisons**: She is most often compared to Bai Lu (similar "sweet but tough" range) and Zhao Lusi (similar sweet-girl-to-leading-lady trajectory). She is generally not compared to Esther Yu, who carries a more glamour-leaning brand.
- **Physicality discourse**: Fans are amused by the contrast between her petite build (the viral "77-pound" livestream moment) and the physical-action scenes in *Pursuit of Jade*. This has become a fandom meme.
- **Dating speculation**: r/CDrama and MDL forum threads have actively debated the Hengdian café spotting and CP-marketing question. No confirmation either way.
