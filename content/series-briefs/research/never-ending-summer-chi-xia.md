## 2. Plot Overview (spoiler-light)

Zhou Wan (周挽), a resilient, top-of-her-class high-schooler, is desperate to fund surgery for her seriously ill grandmother. Her estranged mother — who abandoned her years ago — refuses to help. So Zhou Wan deliberately gets close to **Lu Xixiao (陆西骁)**, the rebellious, troubled stepson of her mother's new family, planning to extract the money through him (in the novel, partly as leverage against her mother).

What begins as calculation turns real: across one searing summer the two wary teenagers fall for each other, repeatedly pulling one another out of personal "free-falls" (the novel's title, *The Fall*, is the central metaphor). But harsh reality and a misunderstanding shatter the young romance and drive them apart. Years later they collide again in the **workplace** as adults — old wounds, revenge impulses, and unextinguished feelings reigniting at once.

### 2a. ENDING — SPOILER SECTION (drama ending NOT yet aired)
- **Drama:** The finale has not broadcast. Promos frame it as a reunion-and-reconciliation arc ("跨越十年" / "across ten years"), strongly implying a reconciliation, but the on-screen resolution is unconfirmed.

After a long separation (novel text describes roughly **seven years** apart; drama marketing says "ten years" — timelines differ), the leads reunite and end up together; in the final chapter (ch. 264) Lu Xixiao pledges to spend the rest of his life with Zhou Wan.

## 4. Character-Name & Title Linguistics (the site's moat)

- **炽夏 Chì Xià** — 炽 *chì* = blazing, ablaze, ardent (same character in 炽热 *chìrè*, "white-hot / fervent"); 夏 *xià* = summer. The title fuses literal scorching summer heat with metaphorical burning passion.
- **周挽 Zhōu Wǎn** — surname 周 *Zhōu*. Given name 挽 *wǎn* = "to pull / draw back / to retrieve / to salvage." A loaded name: she literally tries to **挽** (pull back / save) her grandmother and later the man she loves.
- **陆西骁 Lù Xīxiāo** — surname 陆 *Lù* ("land"). 西 *xī* = "west" (echoes the alias 西风向晚, "west wind toward dusk"). 骁 *xiāo* = "valiant, brave, a spirited/fierce steed" (as in 骁勇 *xiāoyǒng*, "bold and valiant") — fitting for a wild, unbroken-horse type male lead.
