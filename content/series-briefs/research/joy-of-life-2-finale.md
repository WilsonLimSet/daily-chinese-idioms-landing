# Joy of Life Season 2 (庆余年2) Finale — Research Brief

> Research compiled from English- and Chinese-language sources covering the 36-episode drama that aired on Tencent / CCTV May 16 – May 30, 2024. Author: Mao Ni (novel) / screenwriter Wang Juan (王倦). Sources cited inline.

## Episode 32–36 Plot Walkthrough

### Episode 30–31 (set-up — necessary context)

Although the brief asks for eps 32–36, the final arc cannot be understood without two pivotal earlier episodes:

- **Ep 30 — the Hanging Temple ("悬空寺") assassination.** Emperor Qing (庆帝, Qing Di) is targeted by THREE simultaneous assassins atop the Hanging Temple. Fan Xian throws himself between them and is stabbed in the heart with a **poisoned dagger**. The chief assassin is "Shadow" (影子), who is later revealed as Chen Pingping's personal guard and the younger brother of Grandmaster Si Gujian. Conspirators are layered: Qing Di himself engineered the attempt to weaken the Ye family and test Fan Xian's loyalty; Chen Pingping piggy-backed on the plot hoping to help Fan Xian win the Emperor's trust; a vengeful Western Hu/eunuch group joined in. Result: Fan Xian's **internal energy ("真气") is destroyed — he is left a "废人" (martially crippled)** ([163.com](https://m.163.com/dy/article/J3HSHMGH0523SA0G.html), [news.qq.com](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240528A09VNM00)).
- **Ep 30 — the "medical miracle" surgery.** Because the poison has reached his organs, Fan Ruoruo (范若若) — Fan Xian's adopted sister and an amateur physician — performs Qing's first-ever **open-chest surgery** under his conscious guidance (he's only mildly sedated so he can talk her through it). She removes the poisoned tissue and saves his life ([ComingSoon ep30 recap](https://www.comingsoon.net/chinese-drama/news/1746961-joy-of-life-season-2-episode-30-recap-spoilers-zhang-ruoyun-endures-a-fatal-attack), [ChasingDramas ep30 transcript](https://www.chasingdramas.com/transcripts-joy-of-life-s2/joy-of-life-s2-ep-30-medical-miracle/)). Fan Xian survives, but his cultivation is gone.
- **Ep 31 — fallout.** Fan Xian's true parentage spreads to the public. The Empress (皇后) wants him dead because her clan was responsible for Ye Qingmei's death; the Crown Prince urges her to stand down strategically. Gong Dian (宫典), head of the Imperial Guard, is demoted/exiled to Dingzhou for failing to protect the Emperor; Ye Chong (叶重) is exiled with him after submitting eight petitions ([ChasingDramas ep31](https://www.chasingdramas.com/transcripts-joy-of-life-s2/joy-of-life-s2-ep-31-fan-xians-parentage/)).

### Episode 32 — "Marital Disputes"

- Lin Wan'er (林婉儿) confronts Fan Xian after Li Chengze (the Second Prince) tells her Fan Xian killed her brother Lin Gong. Fan Xian admits the **killer was actually Wu Zhu (五竹)**, but that he had known and concealed it. Lin Wan'er, raging, vows to take revenge on Wu Zhu herself; Fan Xian tells her no mortal can defeat a Grandmaster.
- The Empress, dying-ill in storyline terms, pleads with the Emperor to admit her own role and punish Eldest Princess Li Yunrui (李云睿). Qing Di refuses to see her again — he tells her their husband-wife bond is exhausted.
- Qing Di invites Fan Xian to view flowers at the Hanging Temple and has a **decoy dummy in Fan Xian's likeness** built — bait for Wu Zhu ([ChasingDramas ep32](https://www.chasingdramas.com/transcripts-joy-of-life-s2/joy-of-life-s2-ep-32-marital-disputes/)).

### Episode 33 — "Journey to Jiangnan"

- Fan Xian boards the massive official ship to Jiangnan to seize the Three Great Workshops (三大坊) — his mother Ye Qingmei's industrial legacy, currently controlled by the Ming family (明家) and protected by the Second Prince + Eldest Princess.
- Princes Li Chengru, Li Chengze, Li Chengqian and Li Chengping all come to the dock to see him off. Fan Xian publicly declares he is a **Fan family son**, removing himself as a throne contender — Li Chengqian (Crown Prince) is openly relieved.
- Lin Wan'er reconciles enough to wave him off at the dock (an "air hug" — widely GIF'd on Weibo).
- Qing Di assigns the young prince Li Chengping to travel with Fan Xian to Jiangnan. Wang Qinian (王启年) and Shi Chanli are also in the entourage ([CPOP HOME ep 33](https://www.cpophome.com/joy-of-life-season-2-zhang-ruoyun-li-qin/recap/33/), [ChasingDramas ep33](https://www.chasingdramas.com/transcripts-joy-of-life-s2/joy-of-life-s2-ep-33-journey-to-jiang-nan/)).

### Episode 34 — "Making an Entrance"

- Fan Xian arrives at Suzhou. The Suzhou magistrate and a swarm of mid-rank officials throng the dock; Ming Qingda and the Ming Matriarch immediately kneel and publicly apologize.
- Ming Qingda promises that by sundown he'll deliver the person who put a bounty on Fan Xian.
- Fan Xian uses the **130,000 taels** he brought from the capital + the gold/silver bribe-gifts he received en route to send the Jiangbei famine refugees home. The "gifts as bribes" become evidence for later prosecution ([ChasingDramas ep34](https://www.chasingdramas.com/transcripts-joy-of-life-s2/joy-of-life-s2-ep-34-making-an-entrance/), [Taicike ep34](https://men.taicike.com/tv/53895875/fj/11204.html)).

### Episode 35 — "The Depravity of the Ming Family"

This is the episode that defined the Suzhou arc and trended on Chinese social media. Two scenes:

1. **The fake "biological brother" ceremony / Ming Matriarch's murder.** Ming Qingda (明青达) arranges for a Ming-clan cousin to be formally adopted as his "younger brother" so the cousin can be served up to Fan Xian as the lone scapegoat for the bounty. During a private massage scene, **Ming Qingda strangles the Ming Matriarch (老太君) — his own grandmother/matriarch — to death** so she cannot interfere with the cover-up. The matriarch had refused to ask Grandmaster Ye Liuyun (叶流云) to assassinate Fan Xian, calling outside help shameful; Ming Qingda eliminates her dissent ([ChasingDramas ep35 "Depravity of the Ming Family"](https://www.chasingdramas.com/transcripts-joy-of-life-s2/joy-of-life-s2-ep-35-the-depravity-of-the-ming-family/)).
2. **The "willing sacrifice" courtyard massacre.** Fan Xian discovers a courtyard with bodies stacked on carts. Ming Qingda is performing a "kindness" speech to dozens of Ming-family clansmen who, having been promised their families' upkeep, drink poisoned bowls and lie down on cots to die. One man kneels to thank the Ming family as their "great benefactor" before dying. The scapegoat is presented to Fan Xian and then dashes his own head against a column rather than be interrogated. This is widely cited as the **most viscerally disturbing scene of the season** ([ChasingDramas ep35](https://www.chasingdramas.com/transcripts-joy-of-life-s2/joy-of-life-s2-ep-35-the-depravity-of-the-ming-family/)).

### Episode 36 — "Secret Weapon" (大结局)

Three intercut storylines close the show:

**(A) The Three Great Workshops takeover (Jiangnan).** Deng Ziyue (邓子越), the undercover Inspectorate agent Fan Xian planted with the Ming family, brings back the relocated workshops' real address plus a dossier of crimes. Fan Xian raids the workshops. Supervisor Xiao Jing is executed on the spot for bribery, embezzling death pensions, and withholding wages. Supervisor Ma Jianxiong is also beheaded. Supervisor Wu surrenders and helps hand the workshops over. Fan Xian formally takes back his mother's legacy.

**(B) The Wu Zhu vs. Temple Messenger duel (in/near the capital).** A Temple emissary/automaton ("神庙护卫" — the show stages it as a robotic/sci-fi figure with an eyepiece) comes to retrieve Wu Zhu. Wu Zhu initially considers going, but when the emissary threatens Fan Xian, Wu Zhu attacks. They fight "hundreds of rounds" from ground to sky to underwater to mountaintop. Wu Zhu is wounded; he tells Lin Wan'er to relay a message ordering Fan Xian to head south immediately ([qidian ep36](https://m.qidian.com/ziyuan/qingyunian/episode/%E5%BA%86%E4%BD%99%E5%B9%B4%E7%AC%AC%E4%BA%8C%E5%AD%A3%E7%AC%AC36%E9%9B%86%EF%BC%88%E5%A4%A7%E7%BB%93%E5%B1%80%EF%BC%89%E5%88%86%E9%9B%86%E5%89%A7%E6%83%85)).

**(C) Hai Tang Duoduo and the corrected manual.** Fan Xian reveals to Hai Tang Duoduo (海棠朵朵) that the "Tian-Yi Dao" inner-energy manual Master Ku He gave him is sabotaged — practicing it would have kept him below the 9th rank and possibly turned him "idiotic." Hai Tang fixes the errors. Fan Xian admits he also withheld pages from the "Bawang true qi" copy he gave her — mutual hedging between friends ([qidian ep36](https://m.qidian.com/ziyuan/qingyunian/episode/%E5%BA%86%E4%BD%99%E5%B9%B4%E7%AC%AC%E4%BA%8C%E5%AD%A3%E7%AC%AC36%E9%9B%86%EF%BC%88%E5%A4%A7%E7%BB%93%E5%B1%80%EF%BC%89%E5%88%86%E9%9B%86%E5%89%A7%E6%83%85)).

**(D) The Ye Liuyun confrontation (final climax).** Grandmaster Ye Liuyun (叶流云) — one of the Four Grandmasters — has been hired by Eldest Princess Li Yunrui to kill Fan Xian. He arrives by boat at the Suzhou dock. The boat literally flies into the air; Ye Liuyun lands on the dock. Fan Xian, with zero true qi, deliberately does NOT draw his Barrett-style sniper rifle ("巴雷特") — he is gambling that Ye Liuyun cares about the surviving Ye family members. Ye Liuyun flicks his sleeve, sends a dagger flying; Shadow catches it cleanly. Ye Liuyun departs — and as he leaves, his single sword stroke shears off half of a city gate-tower and rips a long fissure into the ground (a famous VFX shot). After he is gone, Fan Xian discovers his **true qi has returned** — Ye Liuyun secretly restored it, repaying a debt he owed Wu Zhu (whom he reveres as the source of his own breakthrough) ([Yahoo / ComingSoon ep36](https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/joy-life-season-2-episode-095753952.html), [163.com 大结局](https://m.163.com/dy/article_cambrian/J3FNI6QP0553JRJ5.html), [Zhihu 大结局](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/701610544), [Sohu](https://www.sohu.com/a/783112341_120843299)).

**(E) The framing-device coda.** The story cuts back to the present-day frame: Professor Ye (modern-day) finishes reading "Zhang Qing's" manuscript and says she still has many questions and is impatient for Joy of Life Season 3. **THE END** ([qidian ep36](https://m.qidian.com/ziyuan/qingyunian/episode/%E5%BA%86%E4%BD%99%E5%B9%B4%E7%AC%AC%E4%BA%8C%E5%AD%A3%E7%AC%AC36%E9%9B%86%EF%BC%88%E5%A4%A7%E7%BB%93%E5%B1%80%EF%BC%89%E5%88%86%E9%9B%86%E5%89%A7%E6%83%85)).

## The Suzhou Massacre Arc Resolution

The arc is set up early in S2 with Fan Xian's appointment to head the Inspectorate (鉴查院) and recover the Imperial Treasury (内库). The Ming family — the Eldest Princess's and Second Prince's commercial proxies — controls the Three Great Workshops, Ye Qingmei's industrial legacy, and is the operational source of treasury corruption.

Set-up beats:
- Fan Xian sends Deng Ziyue undercover as a refugee, infiltrating the Ming family with a forged identity (eps 27–33).
- The Ming family secretly issues a **bounty on Fan Xian** and uses Jiangbei refugees to hijack his treasury ship (eps 33–34).
- Ming Qingda offers a scapegoat at dinner; Fan Xian sees through it (ep 34–35).

Resolution beats (ep 35–36):
- The Ming Matriarch refuses to call in Grandmaster Ye Liuyun; Ming Qingda kills her himself (ep 35).
- The "willing sacrifice" courtyard massacre exposes the Ming family's cult-like control (ep 35).
- Deng Ziyue smuggles out the workshops' real address (ep 36).
- Fan Xian raids the workshops, executes two supervisors, and the third surrenders (ep 36).
- The **larger Ming family / Eldest Princess / Second Prince network is NOT fully dismantled in S2**. Ming Qingda is still alive, the Eldest Princess and Second Prince are alive and active, and the Ming family's full reckoning is explicitly held over for Season 3 ([163.com ending analysis](https://m.163.com/dy/article_cambrian/J3FNI6QP0553JRJ5.html)).

## Fan Xian's Status at the End

**Explicit answer: Fan Xian is ALIVE and his martial cultivation has been secretly restored.**

- He is NOT faking death (that was the Season 2 OPENING premise, not the close).
- He is NOT critically injured at the very end — but he spent most of episodes 30–35 as a "废人" (cultivation-destroyed cripple) after the Hanging Temple incident.
- At the close of ep 36, Ye Liuyun has covertly restored his true qi (真气).
- He has just successfully seized the Three Great Workshops in Suzhou.
- He is heading back to deal with the Wu Zhu / Temple situation per Wu Zhu's relayed message.

## The Cliffhanger

The closing sequence has multiple unresolved threads, but the **final on-screen moment** is the framing device: modern-day Professor Ye finishes Zhang Qing's manuscript and says she has many lingering questions and hopes Season 3 arrives soon — a meta-cliffhanger directed at the audience ([qidian ep36 plot](https://m.qidian.com/ziyuan/qingyunian/episode/%E5%BA%86%E4%BD%99%E5%B9%B4%E7%AC%AC%E4%BA%8C%E5%AD%A3%E7%AC%AC36%E9%9B%86%EF%BC%88%E5%A4%A7%E7%BB%93%E5%B1%80%EF%BC%89%E5%88%86%E9%9B%86%E5%89%A7%E6%83%85)).

The **dramatic cliffhanger** (the one Chinese viewers actually discuss) is the Ye Liuyun pier scene: Fan Xian's gamble that he won't be killed, the world-cleaving sword stroke that destroys half the gate-tower, the silent revelation that his cultivation is back — and the realization that Wu Zhu has been wounded somewhere off-screen, with the Temple itself entering the story as a new antagonist.

Unanswered questions left for Season 3:
1. **Wu Zhu vs. the Temple.** What is the Temple? Why did it send an automaton/messenger for Wu Zhu? This is the **central mystery of S3** per multiple analyses ([sina blog 4大悬念](https://blog.sina.com.cn/s/blog_4a302c450102zpi6.html), [360doc 七大伏笔](http://www.360doc.com/content/24/0605/16/83185678_1125400546.shtml)).
2. **The Battle of Dadong Mountain (大东山之战).** Multiple Chinese commentators name this as the S3 opener — a long-planned showdown between Qing Di and the Grandmasters that, in the novel, leads to the collapse of his alliance with Fan Xian ([sina 4大悬念](https://blog.sina.com.cn/s/blog_4a302c450102zpi6.html)).
3. **Crown Prince + Eldest Princess affair.** Hinted but not exposed in S2 ([sina 4大悬念](https://blog.sina.com.cn/s/blog_4a302c450102zpi6.html)).
4. **Fan Xian–Qing Di relationship.** Both still pretending; eventual conflict telegraphed ([sina 4大悬念](https://blog.sina.com.cn/s/blog_4a302c450102zpi6.html)).
5. **Ming family / Three Great Workshops endgame.** Ming Qingda alive; Eldest Princess and Second Prince network not yet broken ([163.com 大结局](https://m.163.com/dy/article_cambrian/J3FNI6QP0553JRJ5.html)).

## Major Viewer Debate Points

- **Douban score dropped from S1's 7.9 to S2's 7.0–7.2.** S2 closed at ~7.2 with 26.8% five-star, 31.3% four-star, 23.6% three-star, 18.3% one-or-two star out of 160,000+ ratings ([Gamersky 豆瓣 report](https://www.gamersky.com/news/202406/1760088.shtml)).
- **Wang Juan's handling of Lin Wan'er** generated multiple trending Weibo topics; many viewers felt her characterization regressed into "婆媳" / domestic-conflict tropes ([ChasingDramas review](https://www.chasingdramas.com/reviews/currently-watching-joy-of-life-season-2-2024-%E5%BA%86%E4%BD%99%E5%B9%B4%E7%AC%AC%E4%BA%8C%E5%AD%A3/), [澎湃 The Paper](https://www.thepaper.cn/newsDetail_forward_27545464)).
- **Rushed final two episodes.** Recurring complaint: 36 episodes vs. the originally announced 46 ([MDL reviews](https://mydramalist.com/54083-joy-of-life-season-2/reviews); [Drama Slot](https://dramaslot.com/joy-of-life-s2-review/)).
- **Pulling forward novel-ending material** (e.g. Qing Di being a Grandmaster) was divisive — purists felt S2 burned through reveals that should have anchored later seasons.
- **Diminished Yan Bingyun, Hai Tang, Wu Zhu, Fei Jie screen time** ([MDL CostumeHEA review](https://mydramalist.com/profile/Cdrama_costume_addict/review/364085)).
- **Ep 35 massacre scene** ("明家集体服毒"/"明老太君之死") trended on Douyin and Weibo as the season's darkest moment, drawing both praise (for tonal courage) and criticism (for gratuitousness) — a clip routinely cited by Chinese reviewers as the most discussed scene of the run.
- **"叶流云一剑斩半楼" (Ye Liuyun's one-sword half-building cut)** trended as the most-shared VFX moment of the finale ([163.com 大结局](https://m.163.com/dy/article_cambrian/J3FNI6QP0553JRJ5.html), [Zhihu 大结局](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/701610544)).

## Verification Flags

The following claims appeared in some sources but I could NOT confirm with high confidence:

1. **CONTESTED: Deaths of Second Prince, Crown Prince, Empress, Eldest Princess in S2.** Multiple Chinese aggregator articles ([news.qq 二皇子结局](https://news.qq.com/rain/a/20240522A0AYRV00), [Zhihu 两个皇子自杀](https://zhuanlan.zhihu.com/p/700946237), [163.com 十人十种结局](https://c.m.163.com/news/a/J2SFIG9R05525O0R.html)) describe the Second Prince's poison-suicide, Crown Prince's death, Empress's wall-leap and Eldest Princess's suicide as if part of S2. **However, the Chinese Wikipedia plot summary, the qidian official ep-36 synopsis, all English episode recaps, and one Chinese article explicitly noting "这些剧情预计将在第三季呈现"** all indicate these deaths are NOVEL/Season-3 material, NOT shown in S2. The most likely explanation is that the aggregator articles were summarizing the novel/expected S3 events for SEO. I have marked all four characters as **ALIVE at end of S2** in the table above and consider that the safer-confidence position. **Recommend the article not state these characters die in S2.**

2. **PARTIALLY CONFIRMED: Hai Tang gave Fan Xian a "precious manual" that restores his powers.** One English source ([CPOP HOME overall recap](https://www.cpophome.com/joy-of-life-season-2-zhang-ruoyun-li-qin/recap/)) says Hai Tang restores Fan Xian's qi via a manual. The qidian Chinese source for ep 36 instead says Hai Tang **corrects** Fan Xian's existing sabotaged manual; Ye Liuyun is the one who actually restores his qi. I went with the qidian version (more consistent with the Ye Liuyun finale climax across multiple Chinese sources).

3. **UNVERIFIED: Specific viral Weibo hashtags / Douyin clip counts for the Ep 35 massacre and Ye Liuyun sword-stroke.** I can confirm both scenes were heavily discussed and praised/criticized in mainstream Chinese coverage but I could not pull specific Weibo trending-topic numbers or Douyin view counts. Don't quote specific viral metrics without further verification.

4. **UNVERIFIED: Reddit r/CDrama-specific reactions.** Web search returned MyDramaList, JayneStars, Drama Slot, ChasingDramas, and Yahoo reviews, but no direct r/CDrama thread content. The "rushed finale," "long wait for S3," and "Wang Juan changed too much" complaints are amply attested across MyDramaList and Chinese commentary — but treat any quoted Reddit reaction as unsubstantiated.

5. **UNVERIFIED: Exact 2026 Season 3 air date.** Sources variously claim "expected 2026," "expected 2027," and "two-three years." Treat S3 timing as TBD.

6. **TERMINOLOGY NOTE: "Ye Lin Yui" vs "Ye Liuyun."** Multiple English articles transliterate 叶流云 as "Ye Lin Yui" — this appears to be a Pinyin error. The correct romanization is **Ye Liuyun** (叶流云, yè liú yún). Some English-language search returns are ambiguous on this name; use "Ye Liuyun" in the article.

7. **"Ming Qing Da" vs "Ming Qingda."** Same person (明青达). Use Ming Qingda.

8. **Wu Zhu's exact condition.** "Wounded" is well-attested. Whether he is mortally wounded, captured, or merely injured is intentionally ambiguous and unresolved in S2 — the Temple Messenger fight is the S3 hook.
