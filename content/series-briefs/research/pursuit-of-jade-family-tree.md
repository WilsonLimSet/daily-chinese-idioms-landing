# Pursuit of Jade (逐玉) Family Tree & Character Relationships — Research Brief

> Research brief for an SEO article targeting "pursuit of jade family tree" and adjacent GSC queries. Sources triangulated across Wikipedia, MyDramaList discussions, the fan wiki at pursuitofjade.wiki, Newhanfu deep-dives, Sportskeeda/Soap Central recap guides, and Tonboriday's relationship chart. Where sources conflict, the article should flag rather than assert.

Pursuit of Jade is a 40-episode 2026 Tencent/iQIYI/Netflix C-drama directed by Zeng Qingjie, starring Zhang Linghe and Tian Xiwei, adapted from Tuanzi Laixi's (团子来袭) web novel 《逐玉》. It is set in the fictional Dayin (大胤) dynasty, a costume composite of Tang/Song/Ming. The plot pivots on a sixteen-to-seventeen-year-old conspiracy known in-universe as **the Jinzhou Case (锦州案)**: the engineered massacre of a frontier garrison that wiped out the Marquis Wu'an house and erased a rival faction at court. The drama's emotional spine is the matrilocal (入赘) marriage of Fan Changyu, an orphaned butcher's daughter, and Xie Zheng, the disguised sole survivor of that massacre. Roughly seven family/political clusters touch each other, and "how everyone connects" is therefore better understood as a *network of overlapping wounds* than a simple genealogy.

## The Xie Family (Marquis Wu'an's Lineage)

The Xie house is the central tragedy. It is also the smallest family in the drama — by Episode 1, only one Xie remains alive.

- **Xie Linshan (谢临山)** — General, late Marquis Wu'an, Xie Zheng's father. A senior frontier commander of Great Yin. Killed at the Jinzhou battle ~16–17 years before the story when reinforcements failed to arrive (Newhanfu calls him "Marquis of Ningyang" in one breakdown, which is a name variant for the same Wu'an title — flag this).
- **Madam Xie (the late Marquise / 谢夫人)** — Xie Zheng's mother. *Sister of Prime Minister Wei Yan*, which is the load-bearing line in the whole drama. After her husband's death she sent young Xie Zheng out of the house to fetch osmanthus for cakes and then took her own life, leaving a suicide note. She entrusted Xie Zheng to her brother Wei Yan before dying.
- **Xie Zheng (谢征) / Marquis Wu'an (武安侯)** — The lead, played by Zhang Linghe. Around five years old at the time of the massacre, raised inside the Wei household by his maternal uncle. As an adult he reclaims the marquisate, hides his identity in the early arc (going by a scholar persona, with sources also rendering his alias as "Yan Zheng"), and slowly investigates the Jinzhou case from inside the Wei faction that raised him. By the finale he is promoted to **Prince Regent**.
- **Xie Qi (谢起)** — Sometimes written as Yan Qi in early subtitle leaks. Xie Zheng's most loyal aide, sworn brother, and bodyguard; functionally an adopted Xie, not a blood relative. He is the carrier-pigeon contact when Xie Zheng is hiding wounded at the Fan house. Played by Ren Hao (per some sources; cast lists vary — see Verification Flags).

**Extended kin via the Wei marriage:**
- **Madam Wei** — Wei Yan's wife. Xie Zheng is her nephew-by-marriage; multiple sources note she is his only blood-tie maternal aunt-figure in court.
- **Wei Xuan (韦煊?)** — Wei Yan's son, Xie Zheng's cousin. He survives long enough to take an arrow meant for Xie Zheng in the back half of the show, a redemption beat for the Wei line.

**Bottom line:** the Xie lineage is functionally extinguished. The "Xie family" as it operates in the present-tense plot is really one orphan + one cousin (Wei Xuan, half-Wei) + one sworn brother (Xie Qi). Everything else is memory and inheritance.

## The Fan Family (Fan Changyu's Household)

The Fan family is small, lower-class, and shaped by a buried noble secret.

- **Fan Erniu (范二牛)** — Fan Changyu's father. A butcher in a provincial town. Murdered (with his wife) by bandits before Episode 1. His secret, revealed mid-drama, is that he was *not actually born a butcher* — he was the son of **Wei Qilin (韦麒麟/魏麒麟)**, a general scapegoated for the Jinzhou disaster. After his father was branded a traitor, Fan Erniu assumed a new identity and raised his daughters as commoners. He left a suicide note asking General He to protect them.
- **Meng Lihua (孟丽华)** — Fan Changyu's mother. Also killed in the bandit attack. Sources give limited detail; she is named in the Wikipedia character network but not in most fan summaries.
- **Fan Changyu (范长玉)** — The lead, played by Tian Xiwei. Eldest daughter. Wields a butcher's knife, possesses near-supernatural physical strength, supports the household. After the death of her parents she becomes head of family and arranges the matrilocal marriage. Eventually titled "Grand General, First-Rank Lady Protector" (rendering varies); rises to fourth-rank military official mid-show, then general after killing Prince Changxin. Her birth name pun (changyu = "long jade") is the title's payoff.
- **Fan Changning (范长宁)** — Fan Changyu's much-younger sister, often called Ningning. The household's youngest. Promised in childhood-pinky-swear to Yu Bao'er when she is too young to understand; the betrothal is preserved by Yu Qianqian on the condition that he ask her again at marriageable age. Eventually becomes Empress of Great Yin.

**Found family inside the Fan household:**
- **Mr. & Mrs. Zhao (赵伯/赵婶)** — Neighbors who suggest the matrilocal marriage when Changyu's situation gets desperate. Treated as godparents by Changyu.

**No widowed sister-in-law or nephew/niece appears in any source I could verify.** That detail in the prompt may be conflated with another drama or with Mrs. Zhao's neighborly role; flag for the writer.

**Financial/social standing:** lower commercial class — owns a butcher stall, has enough to put a neighbor boy (Song Yan) through school, but no land, title, or guild protection. The Wei Qilin reveal is therefore a class inversion as well as a vendetta reveal: Changyu is *technically* a general's granddaughter, which is why she can plausibly receive a fourth-rank military post once the Jinzhou case is reopened.

## The Imperial Court Hierarchy

This is where the drama gets dense. Several rulers, dead and living, must be tracked across two generations.

**Generation 1 (16–17 years before the story):**
- **Emperor Qi Yi (齐屹)** — The "late Emperor." Sources at Newhanfu name him as *the actual mastermind* of the Jinzhou massacre — paranoid about his own military and his own son.
- **Crown Prince Chengde (承德太子)** — Qi Yi's son and heir, the **father of Qi Min**. Died at Jinzhou alongside General Xie Linshan. He is the missing center of the entire conspiracy: the massacre's real purpose was to kill him.

**Generation 2 (the show's present tense):**
- **Emperor Qi Sheng (齐笙/齐盛)** — Qi Yi's younger son, the current Emperor when the show opens. Installed in the post-Jinzhou power vacuum, increasingly mentally unstable, ultimately forced to abdicate. He is the one who arranges/disrupts marriages by imperial decree, including derailing the Wei daughter–Xie Zheng arranged marriage by substituting Fan Changyu as the bride.
- **Prince Changxin (长信王)** — A rebel prince. Functions as the drama's overt military antagonist for the back arc, the one Xie Zheng/Fan Changyu confront on the battlefield. Sources confirm **Fan Changyu personally kills Prince Changxin** in the finale arc and is promoted to general for it. His exact birth-relationship to Qi Sheng is described inconsistently across sources — he is referred to as a "rebel prince" who promises to share the throne, suggesting he is a brother or close cousin of Qi Sheng rather than his son. Flag for verification.
- **Princess Qi Shu (齐姝)** — Grand Princess, played by Yu Zhongli. Sister or half-sister to the Emperor. Disguises herself as a military physician to follow Gongsun Yin (the man she loves) into camp. Marries Gongsun Yin by the finale. Some sources call her engaged-to-Li Huai'an, which appears to be a mid-show plot beat that is reversed before the end (see Verification Flags).
- **Qi Min (齐珉) / "Sui Yuanhuai" (隋元怀)** — The drama's true endgame villain, played by Deng Kai. Publicly presents as a wealthy rice merchant courting Yu Qianqian; secretly the **son of the dead Crown Prince Chengde**, raised in hiding after his father's death at Jinzhou. He has been impersonating "Sui Yuanhuai" for ~17 years. He orchestrates the rebellion to retake the throne, betrays Great Yin to the Beijue tribes, kills his nominal stepbrother Sui Yuanqing's mother, kills Sui Yuanqing, and ultimately dies by Yu Qianqian's hand (poison delivered as mercy after he poisons himself willingly).
- **Sui Yuanqing (隋元清)** — The "real" Sui heir; Qi Min's foil. Killed by Qi Min after passing the Jinzhou-era tiger tally to Shi Sanniang.
- **Sui Tao (隋涛)** — Qi Min's adoptive father, supports the rebellion.
- **Yu Bao'er / Qi Yu (俞宝儿 / 齐宇)** — A child for most of the show. Biological son of Yu Qianqian and Qi Min. Crowned king/young Emperor after Qi Sheng abdicates. Marries Fan Changning when both come of age.

## Prime Minister Wei Yan and the Conspiracy

This is the architecture of the 16-year feud, and it has three layers — which is why so many viewers find it confusing.

- **Wei Yan (魏炎 / 韦彦)** — Prime Minister of Great Yin, played by Yan Yikuan. Sources transliterate his given name several ways (魏炎 / 韦烟 / 韦彦) and the surname swap between Wei (魏) and Wei (韦) appears in different summaries — flag this for the writer. Functionally: Xie Zheng's *maternal uncle*, brother of the late Madam Xie, the man who raised Xie Zheng after the Jinzhou tragedy.
- **Layer 1 — surface villain.** For the first 25+ episodes, Wei Yan reads as the architect: he gave Wei Qilin a falsified tiger tally, he refused to send reinforcements through Prince Changxin, he benefited politically when both the Xie and Wei Qilin families fell. He covered up the truth for 16 years.
- **Layer 2 — confession in the cell.** Captured and facing execution, Wei Yan admits guilt but reveals he was *not* the prime mover. The late **Emperor Qi Yi** manipulated Wei Yan's careless words into a pretext, then orchestrated the abandonment of the Jinzhou garrison to kill his own son Crown Prince Chengde. Wei Yan is the executor; Qi Yi is the mastermind. Newhanfu calls this "the obvious villain is merely a scapegoat" — a structurally similar plot to *Nirvana in Fire*.
- **Layer 3 — the cover-up.** The current Emperor Qi Sheng inherited his father's secret and the political settlement, which is why he resists reopening the case. The Wei faction's continued protection of him is the present-tense conspiracy. Prime Minister Wei Yan dies via poison (mercy granted by Xie Zheng); Emperor Qi Sheng is forced to abdicate.
- **Romantic subplot inside the conspiracy.** Wei Yan was in love with **Consort Qi**, who was killed by Emperor Qi Yi (some sources say Qi Sheng). They never married; she was used as a pawn. This is a key motive layer that fans flag as the closest thing the show gives Wei Yan to a sympathetic backstory.

## The Marriage Core: Fan Changyu × Xie Zheng

The matrilocal marriage (入赘) is the structural choice that makes everything else legible. By marrying *into* Fan Changyu's household, Xie Zheng:

1. Vanishes from court rolls — perfect cover.
2. Inverts the noble/commoner power dynamic — Fan Changyu legally owns him, the title is hers to confer.
3. Triggers the contract-to-real-love trope that fan threads identify as the show's emotional engine.

**Inner circle around the marriage:**
- **Yu Qianqian (俞浅浅)** — Fan Changyu's best friend, played by Kong Xue'er. Owner of Yixiang Restaurant. Anachronistically savvy (a fan-noted trait that hints at a possible transmigration backstory, though sources don't confirm). She is the matchmaker, confidante, and political adviser of the female lead, and the romantic interest of Qi Min on the male side. Becomes **Empress Dowager Mingde** after her son Yu Bao'er takes the throne.
- **Gongsun Yin (公孙吟) / Li Huai'an (李怀安)** — Played by Li Qing. Headmaster of Luyuan/Liu Yuan Academy in Heijian. Xie Zheng's sworn brother and chief military strategist. Some sources collapse "Gongsun Yin" and "Li Huai'an" as the same character (a hidden noble identity); other sources split them. Flag.
- **Wen Kan / Li Huai'an (separate character per some sources)** — Played by Ren Hao. A nobleman who develops one-sided feelings for Fan Changyu. Promoted alongside her after the Jinzhou case is reopened. The naming-conflict between "Li Huai'an the Gongsun alias" and "Li Huai'an the Wen Kan character" is the single most confused element across English sources. The article should treat them as one character until disambiguated.
- **Xie Qi** — Xie Zheng's right hand inside the marriage household.
- **Mr. & Mrs. Zhao** — godparent figures who brokered the deal.

**Outer ring (rivals/exes):**
- **Song Yan (宋晏)** — Fan Changyu's former fiancé. Educated on her family's money, then broke the engagement after her parents died, citing a fortune-teller's verdict that she was a "star of ill omen." Returns in Ep 35 as a petty official, kneels before her when he learns she is now a general married to a marquis. He carries the social-class revenge beat.

## Supporting Characters & Secondary Romances

- **Princess Qi Shu × Gongsun Yin** — The Grand Princess pursues the headmaster despite the class gap; he initially refuses out of duty. Resolved in marriage. Sources call this the "second couple."
- **Yu Qianqian × Qi Min** — Tragic. He genuinely loves her; she ultimately gives him the mercy poison. The shared child Yu Bao'er ascends the throne. The Newhanfu piece "Why Qi Min Truly Loved Yu Qianqian" treats this as the show's tragic romance.
- **Fan Changning × Yu Bao'er** — Childhood promise becomes legitimate marriage when both come of age. Empress and Emperor pairing.
- **Wei Yan × Consort Qi** — Backstory tragedy, unconsummated, motive for Wei Yan's bitterness.
- **Li Huai'an / Wen Kan → Fan Changyu** — One-sided. He becomes her loyal subordinate.
- **Song Yan ← Fan Changyu (rejected)** — Pure status-inversion beat, not a real triangle.

## Mentor / Disciple Relationships

- **General He (何将军) → Xie Zheng** — Military mentor. Also a close friend of Fan Erniu (Changyu's father); he is the one who *named* Fan Changyu. Dies in battle, leaves a letter asking Lord Li to protect the Fan sisters.
- **Grand Tutor Tao (陶大儒) → Fan Changyu** — Adopts her as goddaughter "Shanjun" (善君). He authenticates the tiger tally and visits Wei Yan in prison. Acts as her political tutor and credentializer at court.
- **Grand Tutor Li (李大儒)** — A counter-mentor. Betrayed Crown Prince Chengde 17 years ago and imprisons his own grandson for protection. Faints when he learns Qi Min is alive and rebelling.
- **Gongsun Yin (Gongsun line) → Li Huai'an / Gongsun Yin himself** — Academy lineage; the Gongsun family runs Luyuan Academy and produces the show's strategist class.
- **Wei Yan → young Xie Zheng** — The most painful pseudo-mentor pair: the man who raised him is also the man who destroyed his father.

The "how everyone connects" insight for the article: **three families orbit one corpse.** Crown Prince Chengde is dead before Episode 1, but every living protagonist is downstream of his murder. Xie Zheng inherits the Xie wound. Fan Changyu inherits the Wei Qilin wound (through her father's hidden identity). Qi Min inherits the Chengde wound directly (as his son). Wei Yan carries the executor's guilt. Emperor Qi Sheng carries the cover-up. The "family tree" Google search is really asking *who shares blame for one event sixteen years ago* — and the answer is almost everyone with a speaking role.

## Verification Flags

These items did **not** cross-verify cleanly and the article should hedge or omit them.

1. **Wei Yan's Chinese name.** Sources give 魏炎, 韦烟, and 韦彦. The pursuitofjade.wiki character page uses 韦彦, which is likely the production-official rendering. Hanyu Pinyin is identical (Wéi Yán) but the character choice differs.
2. **Wei Yan = political rival vs. maternal uncle.** The fan-wiki *character page* describes him as a political rival only; Wikipedia, Sportskeeda, Tonboriday, and multiple Newhanfu pieces all say maternal uncle. **Treat "maternal uncle who raised him" as the verified read.** The wiki page is likely written from an early-episode perspective.
3. **Marquis title spelling.** Wu'an (武安) vs. Ningyang — Newhanfu uses both in different articles. Wu'an is the standard rendering in subtitles.
4. **Wei Qilin surname.** Some sources write 韦麒麟 (matching the Wei Yan family), others 魏麒麟. If 韦麒麟 is correct, the implication is that Fan Changyu and the Wei Yan house share a remote clan — a thematic twist worth flagging but not asserting.
5. **Crown Prince Chengde's exact relationship to Emperor Qi Sheng.** Sources call him "Qi Sheng's brother" implicitly (both sons of Qi Yi). Not confirmed in any single source — inferred.
6. **Prince Changxin's blood relation to the throne.** Called a "rebel prince" who "promises to share the throne." Likely a brother/half-brother or close cousin of Emperor Qi Sheng, but no source states it explicitly. Some readings make him a proxy of Qi Min's faction rather than a true claimant.
7. **Gongsun Yin vs. Li Huai'an.** At least one source (Wikipedia's character network) treats "Gongsun Yin" and "Li Huai'an" as a single character with two names; the fan wiki and Sportskeeda's chart treat them as two characters (Li Qing plays Gongsun Yin, Ren Hao plays Li Huai'an/Wen Kan). **The article should treat them as two characters** unless episode-level evidence emerges.
8. **Xie Qi's actor.** SoapCentral attributes him to "Li Yu Wei," Tonboriday and others attribute him to Ren Hao. Likely Li Yu Wei, with Ren Hao being a different supporting role.
9. **Princess Qi Shu's engagement to Li Huai'an.** Tonboriday's chart says they are engaged; the finale recap pairs her with Gongsun Yin. Likely a mid-show political engagement that is broken before the ending.
10. **The "widowed sister-in-law and nephew/niece" in the Fan household** described in the user prompt could not be verified in any source. The closest analogues are Mrs. Zhao (neighbor godmother) and Fan Changning (younger sister). The article should drop this beat or rephrase as "found family."
11. **Consort Qi vs. Wei Yan's daughter.** Two separate women: Consort Qi (Wei Yan's lost love, killed) and Wei Yan's daughter (the original arranged bride for Xie Zheng, displaced by Fan Changyu). Easy to conflate; flag for the writer.
