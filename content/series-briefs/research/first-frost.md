# The First Frost (难哄) — Research Brief

## Quick facts

- **English title:** The First Frost
- **Chinese title:** 难哄 (Nán Hǒng) — literal: "hard to coax / hard to please / difficult to appease." The title is a single verb, 哄 (hǒng), given the "difficult" prefix 难 (nán). English has no clean one-word rendering; subtitles flip between "hard to coax," "hard to please."
- **Source novel:** 《难哄》 by Zhu Yi (竹已, Zhú Yǐ). Web novel first serialized on Jinjiang Literature City (晋江文学城); print edition December 2020, Jiangsu Phoenix Literature and Arts Publishing House. Genre: contemporary urban romance (都市纯爱).
- **Spinoff relationship — confirmed:** Same "literary universe" as Hidden Love (偷偷藏不住), both by Zhu Yi. Sang Yan (male lead of First Frost) is Sang Zhi's older brother (Sang Zhi is the female lead of Hidden Love). Dramas run parallel / self-contained — you do NOT need to watch Hidden Love first. The drama recast Sang Yan: Victor Ma played him in Hidden Love (supporting role), Bai Jingting plays him as lead in First Frost.
- **Premiered:** February 18, 2025 on Youku (China) + Netflix (worldwide, simulcast)
- **Concluded:** March 10, 2025
- **Episodes:** 32 × 45 minutes
- **Directors:** Qu Youning (also screenwriter), Jiang Ruizhi, Jia Hao. Spelled "Chu Yu Ning" in some sources.
- **Production companies:** Youku Information Technology (Beijing), Wajijiwa, Hunan Galaxy Cool Entertainment
- **Filming:** February–June 2024, primarily in Chongqing (southwestern China); some Hong Kong footage
- **Cast:**
  - Bai Jingting (白敬亭) as Sang Yan (桑延) — bar owner of "Overtime" and programmer
  - Zhang Ruonan (章若楠) as Wen Yifan (温以凡) — reporter
  - Zhang Miaoyi as Zhong Siqiao
  - Edward Chen as Su Haoan
- **MyDramaList score:** 8.9 (38K+ raters), ranked #127 at time of research. IMDb 8.4.
- **Viewership records:**
  - 10,000 "Popularity Heat Index" points within 2 days of Youku release (a record)
  - 1.751 billion views in H1 2025 (Lighthouse/Dengta) — most-watched streaming drama in China first half of 2025
  - Netflix Global Top TV: ranked #6 worldwide on Feb 27, 2025 — HIGHEST-ranked C-drama in Netflix Global daily-ranking history at that time
  - 6.1M Netflix views per user brief (since surpassed by Pursuit of Jade's 7M, April 2026)
- **Awards:** Bai Jingting won a People's Choice Award for Best Actor; nominations at 30th Busan International Film Festival and Asian Television Awards.

## Plot (no major spoilers beyond ep 3)

Wen Yifan, a young reporter, returns to her hometown (fictional Nanwu, visually modeled on Chongqing) for a new job. On her first night back she stops at a bar called "Overtime" (加班) and discovers the owner is Sang Yan — the high school classmate she loved and abandoned six years earlier. Both pretend not to recognize each other.

That pretense collapses quickly. Wen Yifan is harassed by a creepy neighbor; Sang Yan's apartment is damaged by a fire next door. Through logistical coincidence they end up co-renting the same apartment — separate rooms, strangers under one roof, the classic 合租 (hézū, co-renting) trope that's ubiquitous in modern C-drama romance.

Backstory (revealed through episodes 1–3): They'd liked each other in high school. They'd promised to attend Nanwu University together. Yifan broke the promise, vanished, and never explained why. Sang Yan thinks she didn't want him. She actually left because her father had died, her mother remarried and abandoned her to her paternal uncle's household, and her aunt's brother (Che Xing) attempted to sexually assault her. She carries undiagnosed PTSD from that night, manifesting as sleepwalking episodes. The drama's central plot engine: Sang Yan discovering WHY she left — not that she didn't love him, but that she was surviving.

**Setting note:** The cities Nanwu (南梧), Yihe, and Beiyu are fictional but appear in both Hidden Love and First Frost. In Hidden Love, Nanwu is cozy nostalgia. In First Frost, Nanwu is harsher — the same place is a "rigid cage" for Wen Yifan, showing underbelly of harassment and family neglect.

**Who is "hard to coax"?** The title refers primarily to Wen Yifan. She's the one who can't be easily comforted — trauma-armored, overly polite, self-erasing. The drama's engine is Sang Yan learning to coax her anyway — not with grand gestures but with patient, unglamorous daily presence. A secondary reading: Sang Yan is hard to coax back once his heart is broken, and Yifan must re-earn him.

## Cultural context (campus culture, trauma portrayal, 竹已 universe)

**1. The 合租 (co-renting) apartment trope.** In modern China, young adults in first-tier cities almost universally share apartments — rent in Chongqing / Shanghai / Beijing is impossible on entry-level salaries. "Accidentally sharing an apartment with your ex / crush / enemy" is the modern C-drama romance equivalent of the Western "only one bed" trope. It's plausible-realistic (unlike fake-marriage or arranged-marriage setups) and lets characters share domestic intimacy without committing to romantic intimacy.

**2. 复读 / academic-reset stigma.** High school in China is brutal — gaokao prep consumes 3 years. In 2024 over 4 million students retook the gaokao (复读, fùdú). While not explicitly a repeating-grade drama, First Frost leans on the broader emotional logic of Chinese high-school romance: whatever happened in those years is PERMANENT emotional infrastructure. Classmates are formative in a way Western high school rarely is, because the cohort spent 10+ hours a day together for three years.

**3. Trauma portrayal in C-drama is evolving.** Historically, C-dramas papered over mental illness (ghosts, cursed objects, anything supernatural to externalize pain). Post-2022 dramas (Lighter & Princess, Reset, Road Home) started treating PTSD, dissociation, and family abuse clinically. First Frost is part of this shift — Wen Yifan's sleepwalking isn't romanticized "cute clumsy heroine" behavior, it's a diagnosable dissociative trauma response tied to a specific assault memory. The drama doesn't heal it in one dramatic confession; Sang Yan's role is to be patient while she does the work.

**4. 暗恋 (ànliàn, secret crush) as a cultural load-bearing concept.** Western romance treats the "slow burn" as a pacing choice. 暗恋 is a cultural genre of its own — cherished across Chinese literature, music, and film. Years of silent devotion without confession is viewed as RESPECTABLE, romantic, even noble — not pathetic. Both Hidden Love (Sang Zhi's side) and First Frost (Sang Yan's side) are fundamentally 暗恋 stories told from different ends of the family. This is why the Zhu Yi universe resonates so hard: it's 暗恋 canon.

**5. Zhu Yi (竹已) universe.** Zhu Yi is one of the most successful 言情 (yánqíng, romance) authors on Jinjiang. Other novels adapted to dramas: Hidden Love (偷偷藏不住), When I Fly Towards You (向风而行 / also 你是我的荣耀 confusion — verify). Her signature: emotionally restrained male leads, heroines with interior lives, urban realism over fantasy. English fans who loved First Frost often next watch Hidden Love then move through the back catalog.

**6. Name semantics — crucial to articles.**
- 温以凡 (Wēn Yǐfán): 温 = warmth; 以凡 = "through / by the ordinary." Reads as "warmth, shown through ordinariness." A protective name — a wish her parents made that life later broke.
- 桑延 (Sāng Yán): 桑 = mulberry tree (classical symbol of home/hometown, cf. 桑梓 sāngzǐ, "hometown"); 延 = to extend / prolong / continue. "The mulberry that continues" — rooted, enduring, homebound.
- 霜降 (Shuāngjiàng, "Frost's Descent"): Wen Yifan's birthday and nickname. Given by her father, later adopted by Sang Yan. Simultaneously the 18th solar term and her personal identity.
- 败降 (bàijiàng, "surrender / defeat-descent"): a wordplay nickname Sang Yan uses — "I didn't lose to an opponent, I surrendered to you, 温霜降" — pun on 降 (descent / surrender).

## Why it succeeded internationally

**Netflix algorithmic factors:**
- Day-and-date worldwide release (rare for C-drama; usually licensing delays)
- 32 episodes × 45 min = a 24-hour binge — Netflix promotes "bingeable" foreign-language titles
- Bai Jingting's existing international fanbase: Reset (2022, time-loop thriller, 1.8B Tencent views) already had a global cult following; New Life Begins (2022 period rom-com) hit iQIYI fastest-ever-10K-popularity record

**Trope universality:**
- "Childhood friends / former classmates reunited years later" translates cross-culturally (cf. One Day, Normal People, Past Lives)
- Trauma-aware male lead who gives space rather than "saves" the heroine is rare enough to feel fresh to Western audiences tired of alpha-male romance tropes
- Slow burn without fake dating / misunderstandings / makjang overreactions — Western fans specifically praise "no second-lead syndrome"

**How First Frost differs from typical C-drama romance:**
- Modern/urban setting (no palace, no period costumes, no xianxia)
- Realistic pacing — conversations actually happen, trauma isn't resolved in one episode
- No mother-in-law antagonist, no evil other woman, no ex-fiancée arriving to break them up
- PTSD is handled with quiet respect, not hysteria or comic misread
- Production design: Chongqing's mist and neon reads as "cinematic autumn" to Western eyes (visual affinity with Wong Kar-wai aesthetic)

## "Frost" symbolism in Chinese poetry & aesthetic

Frost (霜, shuāng) is one of the most loaded single characters in classical Chinese poetry. It carries, stacked into one syllable: cold, clarity, old age, endurance, loneliness, moral integrity, and the specific Chinese virtue of beauty made sharper by cold.

**Key poetic anchors:**

1. **Li Bai, 《静夜思》 (Quiet Night Thought)** — the most-memorized Chinese poem, taught to every child:
   > 床前明月光，疑是地上霜。
   > 举头望明月，低头思故乡。
   > (Chuáng qián míng yuè guāng / yí shì dì shàng shuāng / jǔ tóu wàng míng yuè / dī tóu sī gùxiāng.)
   > Before my bed, the bright moonlight — I thought it was frost on the ground. I raise my head to gaze at the bright moon; I lower my head and think of home.
   This poem does the cultural heavy lifting: moonlight mistaken for frost = the chill of homesickness, the clarity of memory, and the visual of something cold and ephemeral that looks beautiful. First Frost's title inevitably activates this entire emotional register for Chinese viewers — homesickness, longing, bittersweet return.

2. **霜降 (Shuāngjiàng, Frost's Descent)** — 18th of the 24 solar terms (二十四节气). Runs ~Oct 23–Nov 7. Last term of autumn; first visible frost; transition into winter. UNESCO intangible cultural heritage (2016). Traditional foods: persimmons (柿子) and lamb. Saying: "霜降吃柿子，不会流鼻涕" — eat persimmons at Frost's Descent and you won't get a runny nose. Proverb: "一年补透透，不如补霜降" — a year of tonics doesn't equal a proper Frost's-Descent tonic. Wen Yifan's birthday = 霜降; the whole drama is emotionally set in this "last moment before winter" register.

3. **Frost vs snow in Chinese aesthetic:**
   - 雪 (xuě, snow): soft, accumulating, blanketing, pretty, fleeting-but-social (snow falls on everyone)
   - 霜 (shuāng, frost): crystalline, solitary, appears silently overnight, touches only exposed surfaces
   - Snow is a crowd; frost is a loner. Frost suits a heroine who looks composed but is secretly hurt, whose beauty is literally her defense mechanism.

4. **Du Mu, Su Shi** also used frost imagery heavily for themes of solitary perseverance and late-autumn clarity. (ChineseIdioms.com already has an article on this — "Frost in Classical Chinese Poetry: The Literary DNA of First Frost.")

## Chinese quotes from the drama (10-15)

Verify each against episodes before publishing — several are high-circulation but sourcing is fan-compiled, not official:

1. 这么多年，我还是只喜欢你。
   *Zhème duō nián, wǒ háishì zhǐ xǐhuān nǐ.*
   "After all these years, I still only like you." — Sang Yan (famous rain-under-umbrella confession).

2. 你无声无息地消失，才是真正毁掉我的事。
   *Nǐ wúshēng-wúxī de xiāoshī, cái shì zhēnzhèng huǐdiào wǒ de shì.*
   "Your vanishing without a word — that's what actually destroyed me." — Sang Yan.

3. 我想比你多活六年，这样就能多爱你六年，咱俩就扯平了。
   *Wǒ xiǎng bǐ nǐ duō huó liù nián, zhèyàng jiù néng duō ài nǐ liù nián, zán liǎ jiù chě píng le.*
   "I want to outlive you by six years, so I can love you six more years — then we'll be even." — Wen Yifan (evens out the six lost years).

4. 你这姑娘，怎么这么难哄啊。
   *Nǐ zhè gūniang, zěnme zhème nán hǒng a.*
   "This girl — why are you so hard to coax?" — Sang Yan (title drop).

5. 败降不是败将，而是败给你温霜降。
   *Bàijiàng bù shì bàijiàng, ér shì bài gěi nǐ Wēn Shuāngjiàng.*
   "'Defeat-descent' isn't 'defeated general' — it's that I surrendered to you, Wen Shuangjiang." — Sang Yan (pun on 败将/败降/霜降).

6. 是我亲手抓住了你的阴影，从此以后，你的世界就只剩光了。
   *Shì wǒ qīnshǒu zhuā zhù le nǐ de yīnyǐng, cóngcǐ yǐhòu, nǐ de shìjiè jiù zhǐ shèng guāng le.*
   "I caught your shadow with my own hands — from now on, your world has only light left." — Sang Yan.

7. 原来，在这路遥马急的人间，真的有人把爱意藏了好多年。
   *Yuánlái, zài zhè lùyáo-mǎjí de rénjiān, zhēn de yǒu rén bǎ àiyì cángle hǎo duō nián.*
   "Turns out, in this rushed world of long roads and hurried horses, there really is someone who hides love for many years."

8. 既然没法爱上任何人，那就穷极这一生，去爱那个死磕一辈子都还是想拥有的人。
   *Jìrán méi fǎ àishàng rènhé rén, nà jiù qióngjí zhè yīshēng, qù ài nàge sǐ kē yībèizi dōu háishì xiǎng yǒngyǒu de rén.*
   "If I can't fall for anyone else, then I'll spend this whole life loving the one person I'd stubbornly want even after a lifetime." — Sang Yan.

9. 你出生的那一天，对我来说就是一年到头最佳的黄道吉日。
   *Nǐ chūshēng de nà yītiān, duì wǒ lái shuō jiùshì yī nián dào tóu zuìjiā de huángdào jírì.*
   "The day you were born is, to me, the single most auspicious day of the whole year." — Sang Yan (leans on 黄道吉日, traditional almanac lucky days).

10. 我不介意。你慢慢来。我不走。
    *Wǒ bù jièyì. Nǐ màn màn lái. Wǒ bù zǒu.*
    "I don't mind. Take your time. I'm not leaving." — Sang Yan (iconic three-line reassurance).

11. 命中注定的人，终将会再见。
    *Mìng zhōng zhùdìng de rén, zhōng jiāng huì zàijiàn.*
    "People destined by fate will meet again in the end."

12. 我从来没把你当作备胎。
    *Wǒ cónglái méi bǎ nǐ dàngzuò bèitāi.*
    "I never treated you as a backup plan." — Wen Yifan.

13. 除了你，没有人再喜欢我了。
    *Chúle nǐ, méiyǒu rén zài xǐhuān wǒ le.*
    "Except for you, no one loves me anymore." — Wen Yifan.

14. 你要我怎么证明给你看？
    *Nǐ yào wǒ zěnme zhèngmíng gěi nǐ kàn?*
    "How do you want me to prove it to you?" — Sang Yan.

15. 如果你不说出你的愿望，我要怎么替你实现？
    *Rúguǒ nǐ bù shuōchū nǐ de yuànwàng, wǒ yào zěnme tì nǐ shíxiàn?*
    "If you don't tell me your wish, how can I make it come true for you?" — Sang Yan.

## Relevant idioms catalog (15-20)

Each below should be checked against the site's 1001-idiom database — many are likely already indexed. Tag all as relevant to "First Frost" for internal linking.

1. **青梅竹马** (qīng méi zhú mǎ) — "green plums, bamboo horses" / childhood sweethearts. Origin: Li Bai, 《长干行》. Relevance: they weren't literally childhood friends but high-school classmates; the emotional register is identical.

2. **两小无猜** (liǎng xiǎo wú cāi) — "two little ones, no suspicion" / innocent childhood closeness. Paired idiom to 青梅竹马.

3. **一见钟情** (yī jiàn zhōng qíng) — "one glance, feelings concentrate" / love at first sight. Used ambivalently — was it really "one glance" or years of slow-building?

4. **暗恋** (àn liàn) — not a chengyu but essential compound: secret love. The drama's spine.

5. **破镜重圆** (pò jìng chóng yuán) — "broken mirror reunited" / separated lovers reuniting. From the Princess Lechang legend. Directly applicable — they "broke" six years ago.

6. **旧情复燃** (jiù qíng fù rán) — "old love rekindles." Exact trope match.

7. **情有独钟** (qíng yǒu dú zhōng) — "feelings singularly devoted" / having a soft spot for only one person. Sang Yan's entire arc.

8. **心心相印** (xīn xīn xiāng yìn) — "heart-to-heart mutually sealed" / wordless mutual understanding. From Chan Buddhism. Describes them at their best moments.

9. **守口如瓶** (shǒu kǒu rú píng) — "guard mouth like a bottle" / keep secrets tightly. Wen Yifan's trauma concealment.

10. **沉默寡言** (chén mò guǎ yán) — "silent, few words." Sang Yan's protective demeanor.

11. **念念不忘** (niàn niàn bù wàng) — "think-think not-forget" / constantly remembering. Both of them, for six years.

12. **无微不至** (wú wēi bù zhì) — "not a smallest detail unreached" / attentive to every tiny thing. Sang Yan's daily-care mode of love.

13. **持之以恒** (chí zhī yǐ héng) — "hold it with constancy" / sustained perseverance. The six-year wait and the coaxing.

14. **相濡以沫** (xiāng rú yǐ mò) — "moistening each other with spit" (fish in a drying pond) / mutual support through hardship. Classic Zhuangzi idiom. Fits their co-renting phase.

15. **日久生情** (rì jiǔ shēng qíng) — "days long, feelings arise" / affection that grows over time. The co-renting arc.

16. **一往情深** (yī wǎng qíng shēn) — "deep feeling flowing forward" / devoted love.

17. **刻骨铭心** (kè gǔ míng xīn) — "carved in bone, inscribed on heart" / unforgettably deep. Wen Yifan's trauma AND her love both fit.

18. **知冷知热** (zhī lěng zhī rè) — "knows your cold, knows your warm" / deeply attentive. Frost/warmth resonance with the title.

19. **冰清玉洁** (bīng qīng yù jié) — "ice-clear, jade-pure." Frost-adjacent; virtue-through-cold aesthetic.

20. **守得云开见月明** (shǒu dé yún kāi jiàn yuè míng) — "wait until the clouds open to see the bright moon" / patience rewarded. Exact payoff structure of the drama.

## Fact-check flags

1. **Netflix viewership number (6.1M):** The user-provided 6.1M Netflix view count is plausible but not directly confirmed by Netflix (Netflix doesn't typically publish per-title view counts at this granularity for non-English titles unless in Top 10 lists). Wikipedia confirms #6 Global Top TV Feb 27, 2025 and "highest-ranked C-drama in Netflix Global daily ranking history." Use cautiously — phrase as "reportedly ~6.1M Netflix views" or cite the Chinese-platform numbers (1.75B H1 2025) which are officially sourced from Lighthouse/Dengta.

2. **"Hidden Love spinoff" framing:** Accurate but nuanced. They're better described as sibling novels / parallel stories in a shared universe by the same author. First Frost's timeline actually BEGINS before Hidden Love chronologically. Avoid "sequel" — use "companion novel" or "sister story" or "parallel drama."

3. **Actor recast:** Victor Ma played Sang Yan in Hidden Love (a minor supporting role). Bai Jingting took over for First Frost (lead role). Both are canon Sang Yan — it's a recast, not a different character.

4. **Zhu Yi (竹已) backlist:** Verify exact list of her adaptations before claiming "When I Fly Towards You" is hers — there's some confusion in fan write-ups. Hidden Love (偷偷藏不住) is definitely hers; First Frost (难哄) is definitely hers. Cross-check the rest on Jinjiang before claiming in article.

5. **Quote attribution:** All 15 quotes above circulate widely on Baidu, Zhihu, Sohu — but some may blend book lines with drama lines, and book edition vs drama edition of the same scene sometimes differ. For flagship article, verify each quote against the actual episode before publishing, ideally with episode + timestamp.

6. **City name Nanwu characters:** Some sources write 南梧, others imply 南午. The novel doesn't fix a canonical hanzi in some editions — worth verifying with the published novel text before committing to a spelling.

7. **Director credit:** Qu Youning (曲游宁) is credited directly; some English-language sources romanize as "Chu Yu Ning." Pick one, footnote the other.

8. **Episode run:** 32 episodes confirmed. Some streaming platforms split differently — Netflix uses 30 + 2 special, some sources list 30. Use 32.

9. **"Hidden Love spinoff" in article titles:** If using in URL slug / title, safer phrasing is "sister drama" or "companion to Hidden Love" because "spinoff" implies a derivative work, whereas these are co-equal novels by the same author in a shared universe.
