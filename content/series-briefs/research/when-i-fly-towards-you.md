## 1. Title Meaning — 当我飞奔向你

**Literal:** *dāng wǒ fēi bēn xiàng nǐ* — "When I run/sprint toward you" (or "When I dash toward you").

- 当 (dāng) — "when / at the moment"
- 我 (wǒ) — "I"
- 飞奔 (fēi bēn) — literally "fly-run" — to sprint, to dash, to bolt headlong. Stronger and more bodily than 跑 (pǎo). The 飞 ("fly") prefix injects urgency and abandon — it's the speed of someone who has stopped caring how they look.
- 向你 (xiàng nǐ) — "toward you"

**Narrative significance.** The title is a first-person promise from Su Zaizai (苏在在), the sunny, headlong female lead, to Zhang Lurang (张陆让), the withdrawn male lead. Where the source novel was titled *她病得不轻* ("She's a Little Crazy / She's Got It Bad," referring to Su Zaizai's love-at-first-sight obsession), the show retitled to foreground **her agency**: she is the one sprinting, the one who closes the distance. Zhang Lurang has spent his life waiting to be chosen second (his mother favors his younger brother); Su Zaizai is the first person who runs at him. The drama's emotional thesis is that love is **active motion, not passive arrival** — which is why 飞奔 (not 走, not 跑) is the right verb.

**Why it's evocative.** The conditional 当 ("when") implies a recurring vow, not a one-time event — every time I see you, I run. Pair that with the show's most-quoted line — "*当我飞奔向月亮的时候，月亮也在为了我学习奔跑*" ("When I sprint toward the moon, the moon is also learning to run for me") — and the title becomes the show's entire emotional engine: mutual, accelerating, headlong love between two people who decide to meet each other at speed.

## 2. Director Mao Deshu / Cattree (猫的树) Context

**Real name:** Yuan Shuang (袁爽). **Born:** February 22, 1991, Nanjing, Jiangsu. **Pen name 猫的树** literally "cat's tree" — soft, whimsical, off-center, which is exactly the brand.

**Aesthetic signature.** Cattree is the closest thing C-drama has to a recognized **author director of fresh-pastel youth romance**. Industry coverage (Sohu, Sina, Douban reviews) repeatedly uses the phrase "**少女心**" (*shàonǚ xīn*, "girl's heart") to describe his lens — and he embraces the label. Recurring marks:

- High-key natural light, soft-focus close-ups, lens flares used as emotional punctuation
- Pastel color grading (sky blues, dusk pinks, paper-yellow classrooms) — never the grey-teal "premium drama" palette
- Loose, ensemble blocking — friends shot together at eye level, not power-dynamic asymmetry
- Slice-of-life pacing — long scenes about nothing (sharing earbuds, walking home, watching rain), which is where the romance actually lives
- **Music-led emotional beats** — he edits to song bridges, not dialogue

**Filmography link to Love Between Lines (2026).** Cattree directed **Love Between Lines / 轧戏** (Jan 2026, Chen Xingxu + Lu Yuxiao). It's a Republican-era × modern romance with a jubensha (剧本杀) double-life premise — superficially a genre pivot, but reviewers note the same fresh-pastel palette, music-led editing, and ensemble warmth carry over. **For SEO purposes this is the cross-link: same director, same emotional fingerprint, four years apart.** A reader who searches "Love Between Lines director" should land on the *When I Fly Towards You* article and vice versa.

Other works in his filmography include *Let's Meet Now* (2022) and additional iQiyi 恋恋剧场 slot dramas. The Cattree brand is **iQiyi/Youku romance theater**, not satellite-TV prestige.

## 3. Plot Summary

**Setting.** Early autumn 2012, **Jiangyi High School** (江翼高中). The drama follows a five-person friend group from senior-year high school through ten years of adulthood — but the emotional core stays in the high-school timeline.

**Premise.** Su Zaizai (苏在在), a cheerful transfer student, arrives on the first day of school and spots a boy walking through the rain without an umbrella — Zhang Lurang (张陆让), the top student of the grade, cold and emotionally walled off. She falls in love at first sight and decides — openly, repeatedly, with zero shame — that she's going to win him over.

**Central romance.** Su Zaizai is the **sun** of Zhang Lurang's life. He grew up with a mother who openly favored his younger half-brother Zhang Luli (张陆离); his entire personality is the brittle perfection of a child who decided that if he was the best student, he'd be hard to ignore. Su Zaizai disassembles that — not by fixing him, but by deciding he was already worth running toward before he'd done anything to earn it. Their relationship is built on **peer tutoring** (she's good at English, he's good at math), late-night phone calls, and a slowly-collapsing emotional perimeter.

**Key conflicts.**
1. Zhang Lurang's family — the favoritism toward his brother, his strained relationship with his mother, and the slow process of him allowing Su Zaizai to actually see that wound
2. The college entrance exam (高考) — both the academic pressure and the geography of where each character will be afterward
3. **The 10-year time skip** — a second-act jump from high school to adulthood, where the friend group has fractured and reformed and the romance has to be re-chosen as adults, not as kids
4. **Notably absent:** the show is praised for **refusing toxic tropes** — no love triangle that matters, no big misunderstanding, no third-act breakup engineered by an outside party. Conflict is internal, not melodramatic.

**Tone.** Coming-of-age romantic comedy. 24 episodes × 30 minutes — a tight, light shape.

## 5. Source Novel

- **Title:** 《她病得不轻》 ("She's a Little Crazy" / "She's Got It Bad")
- **Author:** 竹已 (Zhú Yǐ / "Zhu Yi") — one of the most consistent campus-romance IP authors of the 2018–2023 wave
- **Platform:** Originally serialized on **Jinjiang Literature City** (晋江文学城, jjwxc.net), the dominant CN web-novel platform for female-oriented romance
- **Why the retitle:** The novel's title centers Su Zaizai's "illness" (her infatuation), framing her as the obsessive one. The show flipped to *When I Fly Towards You*, which is **her chosen, agentic action** rather than a pathologized "crazy." This rephrasing is part of why the show reads more emotionally healthy than the novel's framing suggests.
- **Zhu Yi's broader catalog.** Same author also wrote *偷偷藏不住* (*Hidden Love*, 2023) and *念念有词* — she is a dominant force in the JJWXC campus-romance ecosystem. Cross-link opportunity: readers who like Hidden Love will like When I Fly Towards You and vice versa, **same author IP**.

## 6. Cultural Moat — What Lives On in 2026

This is the SEO-load-bearing section. Past-peak dramas only win on **cultural specifics that outlive the airing window**. For this show, the moat is:

### 6a. Cattree's fresh-pastel high-school aesthetic
Not a sports drama. The setting is academic high school (高考-prep), and the visual signature is:
- **Rainy first encounter** — Zhang Lurang walking through rain without an umbrella, scored to soft piano, is the show's iconic image. This is the scene that lives on Douyin (per Sohu coverage, the "雨中奔跑" mixed cuts have crossed 10 million views on Douyin's 抖音精选).
- **The classroom as a romance set** — peer-tutoring scenes (English/math exchange), shared earbuds, exchanged notebooks, the desk-pair as a unit of intimacy.
- **The Ferris wheel** — a confession/connection scene cited by Baidu Baike as iconic.
- **The bus and the bus stop** — the line "*公交车不等人，等你的是在意你的人*" ("Buses don't wait — but people who care about you do") is one of the show's most-quoted, and it's specifically tied to the school bus stop as a location.

### 6b. The basketball/sports motif — present but secondary
Basketball scenes and a school athletic event do appear (Baidu Baike confirms), and Guan Fang is athletic — but **this is not Skate Into Love or Falling Into Your Smile**. The sport is a setting for friend-group bonding, not the central metaphor. **Do not lean the article on a sports angle** — the cultural moat is the rainy-romance aesthetic and the quote-driven emotional vocabulary, not athletics.

### 6c. The viral monologue / quote economy
The strongest moat. The show is **quote-driven** to a degree most C-dramas are not. The opening narration ("旁白") and Su Zaizai's monologues spawned a still-active Douyin (抖音) ecosystem of 文案 (caption-text) clips — users overlay show quotes on their own footage. The juzikong quote archive and Zhihu line-by-line analyses confirm: this show's afterlife is **literary**, not visual. **The article should be quote-anchored.**

### 6d. The "10-year time skip" emotional payoff
The high-school-to-adulthood timeline is the show's structural innovation in this genre. It's the reason fans return to the show years later — the second-act adult reunion is what makes the high-school portion feel earned in retrospect. This is also why the show plays as "青春封神" (genre-defining youth drama) in 2026 retrospectives, not just as "a 2023 show."

## 8. Memorable Chinese Quotes (Verified)

All eight below are from juzikong's verified quote archive. Numbers 1–3 are the **viral tier** — the ones with active Douyin afterlife.

**Quote 1 (the title-companion line):**
- 中文: 一定要当太阳吗，可以的话，星星、路灯、萤火虫，想发光的时候就发光，不想发光的时候就不发光，总有人能在人群中一眼就找到你。
- Pinyin: *Yīdìng yào dāng tàiyáng ma? Kěyǐ dehuà, xīngxīng, lùdēng, yíng huǒchóng, xiǎng fāguāng de shíhòu jiù fāguāng, bù xiǎng fāguāng de shíhòu jiù bù fāguāng. Zǒng yǒu rén néng zài rénqún zhōng yī yǎn jiù zhǎodào nǐ.*
- English: "Do you have to be the sun? If you'd rather, be a star, a streetlight, a firefly — shine when you want to, don't shine when you don't. Someone will still find you at a glance in the crowd."
- Context: Su Zaizai to Zhang Lurang, dismantling his "I have to be the top student to be seen" wound.

**Quote 2 (the logic-proof love line):**
- 中文: 我喜欢你，不算命题，因为喜欢判断不了真假。如果我存在，那存在我喜欢你。如果我不喜欢你了，那我就不是我。
- Pinyin: *Wǒ xǐhuān nǐ, bù suàn mìngtí, yīnwèi xǐhuān pànduàn bùliǎo zhēnjiǎ. Rúguǒ wǒ cúnzài, nà cúnzài wǒ xǐhuān nǐ. Rúguǒ wǒ bù xǐhuān nǐ le, nà wǒ jiù bùshì wǒ.*
- English: "'I like you' isn't a proposition, because liking can't be judged true or false. If I exist, then 'I like you' exists. If I no longer like you, then I'm no longer me."
- Context: A math/logic exchange repurposed as a love confession — the show's most-shared quote, peak Zhang Lurang voice.

**Quote 3 (the title-explainer line):**
- 中文: 原来每个女孩在喜欢的人面前都是公主。因为当我飞奔向月亮的时候，月亮也在为了我学习奔跑。
- Pinyin: *Yuánlái měi gè nǚhái zài xǐhuān de rén miànqián dōu shì gōngzhǔ. Yīnwèi dāng wǒ fēi bēn xiàng yuèliàng de shíhòu, yuèliàng yě zài wèile wǒ xuéxí bēnpǎo.*
- English: "It turns out every girl is a princess in front of someone she loves — because when I sprint toward the moon, the moon is learning to run for me too."
- Context: The title's own gloss. **This is the line the article should be built around.**

**Quote 4 (the bus stop line):**
- 中文: 公交车不等人，等你的是在意你的人。
- Pinyin: *Gōngjiāo chē bù děng rén, děng nǐ de shì zàiyì nǐ de rén.*
- English: "Buses don't wait for people. The one who waits for you is the one who cares."
- Context: A bus-stop scene; aphoristic, widely screenshot.

**Quote 5 (the cosmic-singularity line):**
- 中文: 宇宙中有两千亿亿颗星星，却找不出两颗一模一样的。
- Pinyin: *Yǔzhòu zhōng yǒu liǎng qiān yì yì kē xīngxīng, què zhǎo bù chū liǎng kē yīmúyīyàng de.*
- English: "There are 20 quintillion stars in the universe — and you can't find two exactly alike." (两千亿亿 = 2 × 10^19; the show's number is poetic, not astronomical — actual estimates run ~10^22-10^24.)
- Context: A "you are irreplaceable" line, scaled to astronomy. Pure Cattree register.

**Quote 6 (the youth memory line):**
- 中文: 属于我们最炽热的青春，就是我悄悄靠近过你，偷偷记录下我们的同框。
- Pinyin: *Shǔyú wǒmen zuì chìrè de qīngchūn, jiùshì wǒ qiāoqiāo kàojìn guò nǐ, tōutōu jìlù xià wǒmen de tóng kuàng.*
- English: "Our most blazing youth was me quietly coming closer to you, secretly recording the frames we shared."
- Context: A retrospective narration line — the show's voice from the adult timeline looking back.

**Quote 7 (the philosophical-comfort line):**
- 中文: 世界上的事情就是这样，山的那一边是海；深夜的尽头是光；不那么可爱的东西，转个身也许会用另一种难忘的方式留在回忆里。
- Pinyin: *Shìjiè shàng de shìqíng jiùshì zhèyàng, shān de nà yībiān shì hǎi; shēnyè de jìntóu shì guāng; bù nàme kě'ài de dōngxī, zhuǎn gè shēn yěxǔ huì yòng lìng yī zhǒng nánwàng de fāngshì liú zài huíyì lǐ.*
- English: "That's how it goes in this world — on the other side of the mountain is the sea; at the end of the deepest night is light; the things that weren't quite lovely may turn around and stay in memory in another unforgettable way."
- Context: A monologue-narration line, very Cattree-coded.

**Quote 8 (the farewell-blessing line):**
- 中文: 希望我最好的朋友们往后平安喜乐，吃好喝好，每天都能够睡个好觉，凡是有选择，所遇之人都热忱，纵使远隔千里，在彼此看不到的时光里也能熠熠生辉。
- Pinyin: *Xīwàng wǒ zuì hǎo de péngyǒumen wǎnghòu píng'ān xǐlè, chī hǎo hē hǎo, měitiān dōu nénggòu shuì gè hǎo jiào. Fánshì yǒu xuǎnzé, suǒ yù zhī rén dōu rèchén, zòngshǐ yuǎn gé qiānlǐ, zài bǐcǐ kàn bù dào de shíguāng lǐ yě néng yìyì shēnghuī.*
- English: "I hope my best friends live in peace and joy from now on — eat well, drink well, sleep well every night. May they always have choices, may the people they meet be warm-hearted, and even separated by a thousand miles, in the hours we can't see each other, may they still shine."
- Context: The friend-group farewell — speaks directly to the 10-year-time-skip emotional arc.
